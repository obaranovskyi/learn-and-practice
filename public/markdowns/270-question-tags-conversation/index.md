# Question Tags in Conversation

Tags used to confirm, engage, or soften statements.

## Functions in Speech

### Seeking confirmation (rising intonation ↗)
- *You're coming, aren't you?* ↗ (genuinely asking)
- *She left, didn't she?* ↗

### Expecting agreement (falling intonation ↘)
- *Nice day, isn't it?* ↘ (statement, not real question)
- *That was good, wasn't it?* ↘

### Engaging/Rapport building
- *Lovely weather, isn't it?*
- *Great party, wasn't it?*

## Conversational Uses

### Softening commands
- *Close the door, will you?*
- *Pass the salt, would you?*

### Expressing surprise
- *So you're married, are you?*
- *She's leaving, is she?*

### Challenging
- *You think you're clever, do you?*
- *So I'm wrong, am I?*

## Common Conversational Tags

### Universal tags (informal)
- *right?* - "You're coming, right?"
- *yeah?* - "She's nice, yeah?"
- *no?* - "That's correct, no?"
- *OK?* - "Meet at 5, OK?"
- *eh?* (Canadian/British)

### Invariant tag
- *innit?* (isn't it) - informal British
- "That's nice, innit?"

## Intonation Matters

- **Rising** ↗ = real question
- **Falling** ↘ = statement/rhetorical
