# Hyphens and Dashes

Three different marks: hyphen (-), en dash (–), em dash (—).

## Hyphen (-)

### Compound adjectives before nouns
- *a well-known author* (but: *the author is well known*)
- *a two-year-old child*
- *full-time job*

### Compound numbers
- *twenty-one*, *ninety-nine*
- *two-thirds*

### Prefixes (sometimes)
- *self-confident*, *ex-wife*
- *re-enter* (to avoid confusion with *reenter*)
- *anti-war* or *antiwar* (varies)

### Word breaks at line end
- *com-*
- *puter*

## En Dash (–)

### Ranges
- *pages 10–20*
- *2010–2020*
- *Monday–Friday*

### Connections
- *the London–Paris train*
- *the score was 3–2*

## Em Dash (—)

### Interruption or emphasis
- *She was happy—truly happy—for the first time.*
- *I need three things—milk, eggs, and bread.*

### Instead of parentheses
- *The result—as we expected—was positive.*

### Abrupt change
- *I was going to say—never mind.*

## Summary
| Mark | Use | Example |
|------|-----|---------|
| - | Compound words | well-known |
| – | Ranges | 10–20 |
| — | Interruption | —as I said— |
