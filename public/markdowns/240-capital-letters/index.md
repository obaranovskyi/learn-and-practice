# Capital Letters

Rules for when to capitalize.

## Always Capitalize

### Beginning of sentences
- *The dog ran away.*

### The pronoun "I"
- *She and I went to the store.*

### Proper nouns (specific names)
- People: *John Smith*, *Dr. Jones*
- Places: *Paris*, *Mount Everest*, *the Pacific Ocean*
- Organizations: *United Nations*, *Microsoft*
- Days/Months: *Monday*, *January* (NOT seasons: spring)
- Holidays: *Christmas*, *Thanksgiving*
- Languages/Nationalities: *English*, *Japanese*, *American*
- Religions: *Christianity*, *Buddhism*

### Titles before names
- *President Biden* (but: *the president*)
- *Dr. Smith* (but: *a doctor*)
- *Professor Brown*

### Titles of works (major words)
- *The Great Gatsby*
- *Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone*

## Don't Capitalize

### Common nouns
- *a city*, *a country*, *a school*

### Seasons
- *spring*, *summer*, *fall*, *winter*

### Directions (unless region names)
- *Go north.* (direction)
- *the North* (region)

### After colons (usually)
- *She had one goal: to win.*
- Exception: *Note: This is important.* (full sentence)
