# Cleft Sentences

Cleft sentences split a sentence to **emphasize** one part.

## It-Clefts

**It is/was + emphasized part + that/who**

Original: *John broke the window.*

Emphasizing different parts:
- ***It was John** who/that broke the window.* (emphasize who)
- ***It was the window** that John broke.* (emphasize what)
- ***It was yesterday** that John broke the window.* (emphasize when)

## What-Clefts (Pseudo-clefts)

**What + subject + verb + is/was + emphasized part**

Original: *I need a vacation.*
- ***What I need** is a vacation.*

Original: *She loves music.*
- ***What she loves** is music.*

## Other Wh-Clefts

- ***The thing** that I need is rest.*
- ***The person** who helped me was John.*
- ***The reason** why I left was the noise.*
- ***The place** where we met is special.*
- ***All** I want is peace.*

## Reversed Wh-Clefts

- ***A vacation** is what I need.*
- ***Music** is what she loves.*

## Uses

1. **Emphasis**: highlight important information
2. **Contrast**: *It was JOHN who did it, not Mary.*
3. **Clarification**: *What I meant was...*

## Negative Clefts

- *It **wasn't** John who broke it.*
- *It's **not** money that I need.*
