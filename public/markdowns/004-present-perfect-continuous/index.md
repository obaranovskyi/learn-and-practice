# Present Perfect Continuous

Emphasizes duration of activity from past to present. Form: **have/has + been + verb-ing**

## Uses

### 1. Duration (How long?)
- I **have been waiting** for an hour.
- How long **have** you **been studying**?

### 2. Recent Activity with Visible Results
- You're tired. **Have** you **been running**?
- Her eyes are red. She **has been crying**.

## vs Present Perfect Simple

| Simple (result/completion) | Continuous (duration) |
|---------------------------|----------------------|
| I've read 3 books. | I've been reading a lot. |
| She's written the report. | She's been writing all day. |

## Don't Use Continuous With:
- Stative verbs: ❌ I've been knowing him → ✅ I've known him
- Counting: ❌ I've been writing 5 emails → ✅ I've written 5 emails
