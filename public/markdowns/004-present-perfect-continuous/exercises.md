# Exercises: Present Perfect Continuous

## Exercise 1: Fill in the Blanks
Complete with Present Perfect Continuous.

1. She ______ (wait) for two hours.
2. How long ______ you ______ (study) English?
3. It ______ (rain) all day.
4. They ______ (not/sleep) well lately.
5. I ______ (try) to reach you since morning.

**Answers:**
1. has been waiting
2. have, been studying
3. has been raining
4. haven't been sleeping
5. have been trying

---

## Exercise 2: Present Perfect Simple or Continuous?

1. I ______ (read) this book for three hours. (still reading)
2. I ______ (read) three books this month. (quantity)
3. She ______ (work) here since 2018. (permanent)
4. She ______ (work) on this report all morning. (duration)
5. How many emails ______ you ______ (write)? (quantity)

**Answers:**
1. have been reading
2. have read
3. has worked
4. has been working
5. have, written

---

## Exercise 3: What Have They Been Doing?
Look at the situations and write what the person has been doing.

1. John is tired. (work hard)
2. Mary's hands are dirty. (garden)
3. Tom smells of smoke. (smoke)
4. Lisa's eyes are red. (cry)
5. The children are wet. (play in the rain)

**Answers:**
1. He has been working hard.
2. She has been gardening.
3. He has been smoking.
4. She has been crying.
5. They have been playing in the rain.

---

## Exercise 4: Correct the Mistakes

1. How long have you been know him?
2. I've been writing five emails today.
3. She has been working here since 5 years.
4. They have been live in Paris since March.
5. I been waiting for an hour.

**Answers:**
1. How long have you **known** him? (know is stative)
2. I've **written** five emails today. (quantity = Simple)
3. She has been working here **for** 5 years.
4. They have been **living** in Paris since March.
5. I **have** been waiting for an hour.

## Exercise 5: Ask Questions

Write questions using "How long...?" and Present Perfect Continuous.

1. you / wait
2. she / work / here
3. they / live / in London
4. he / learn / to drive
5. it / rain

**Answers:**
1. How long have you been waiting?
2. How long has she been working here?
3. How long have they been living in London?
4. How long has he been learning to drive?
5. How long has it been raining?
