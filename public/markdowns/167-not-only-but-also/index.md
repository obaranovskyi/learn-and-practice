# Not only...but also

"Not only...but also" emphasizes that both things are true, with the second being surprising or additional.

## Basic Usage

- *She's **not only** smart **but also** kind.*
- *He **not only** sings **but also** plays guitar.*
- *We visited **not only** Paris **but also** Rome.*

## Emphasis

The second element often receives more emphasis:
- *It's **not only** cheap **but also** high quality!*
- *She **not only** finished early **but also** won the prize!*

## "Also" is Optional

- *He's not only talented **but** hardworking (too).*
- *She speaks not only English **but** French as well.*

## Inversion (Formal)

When starting with "not only", invert the subject and verb:
- ***Not only** does she sing, **but** she also dances.*
- ***Not only** did he win, **but** he also broke the record.*
- ***Not only** is she beautiful, **but** she's also intelligent.*

## Subject-Verb Agreement

Verb agrees with the **nearer** subject:
- ***Not only** the students **but also** the teacher **was** late.*
- ***Not only** he **but also** his friends **were** invited.*

## Parallel Structure

Keep elements parallel:
- ✓ *not only **smart** but also **kind*** (adj + adj)
- ✓ *not only **sings** but also **dances*** (verb + verb)
