# Past Participles (V-ed/V3)

The past participle is the third form of the verb (V3).

## Formation

### Regular Verbs: Add -ed
- *walk → walked*
- *play → played*
- *stop → stopped* (double consonant)

### Irregular Verbs: Various Forms
- *go → gone*
- *write → written*
- *break → broken*
- *see → seen*

## Uses

### 1. Perfect Tenses
- *I **have finished**.*
- *She **had left**.*
- *They **will have arrived**.*

### 2. Passive Voice
- *The book **was written** by her.*
- *The window **has been broken**.*
- *It **will be done**.*

### 3. As Adjectives
- *a **broken** window*
- *the **stolen** car*
- *a **written** report*

### 4. In Participle Clauses (Passive Meaning)
- ***Exhausted** by the journey, he slept.*
- ***Built** in 1900, the house is historic.*
- ***Seen** from above, the city looks small.*

### 5. After "have/get something done"
- *I **had** my car **repaired**.*
- *She **got** her hair **cut**.*

## Past Participle vs Past Simple

- Past simple: action in past → *I walked*
- Past participle: needs auxiliary → *I have walked*
