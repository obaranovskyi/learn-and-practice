# Reference Words

Reference words point back or forward in text.

## Demonstratives

- **This/These**: near (in text or idea)
  - *This problem needs attention.* (just mentioned)
  - *These issues are complex.*

- **That/Those**: far (earlier in text)
  - *That was a mistake.* (mentioned earlier)
  - *Those days are gone.*

## Personal Pronouns

- *John arrived. **He** was tired.*
- *The team worked hard. **They** succeeded.*
- *I read the book. **It** was interesting.*

## Possessives

- *The company announced **its** plan.*
- *Students should bring **their** books.*

## Such / This Kind of

- *Crime is increasing. **Such** problems require action.*
- *This **kind of** behavior is unacceptable.*

## The Former / The Latter

- *I met Tom and Mary. **The former** is a teacher; **the latter** is a doctor.*
- Former = first mentioned; Latter = second mentioned

## Substitution

- **One/Ones**: *I need a pen. Do you have **one**?*
- **Do so**: *He promised to help and **did so**.*
- **The same**: *She ordered coffee. I'll have **the same**.*

## Avoiding Ambiguity

Be clear about what reference words refer to:
- ❌ *Tom told John that he was wrong.* (who is "he"?)
- ✅ *Tom told John that John was wrong.*
