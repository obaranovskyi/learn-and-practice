# Complex Sentences

A complex sentence has **one independent clause** and **one or more dependent clauses**.

## Structure

**Independent Clause + Dependent Clause**
or
**Dependent Clause + Independent Clause**

## Types of Dependent Clauses

### 1. Adverb Clauses (time, reason, condition)
- ***When** I arrive, I'll call you.*
- *I stayed home **because** it rained.*
- ***If** you study, you'll pass.*

### 2. Adjective/Relative Clauses
- *The man **who called** is here.*
- *The book **that I bought** is great.*
- *The place **where we met** is special.*

### 3. Noun Clauses
- *I know **that she's coming**.*
- ***What you said** was true.*
- *Tell me **where you live**.*

## Subordinating Conjunctions

| Type | Conjunctions |
|------|--------------|
| Time | when, while, before, after, until, as soon as |
| Reason | because, since, as |
| Condition | if, unless, provided that |
| Contrast | although, though, even though |
| Purpose | so that, in order that |

## Punctuation

- **Dependent first**: comma after
  - ***When I arrived**, I called.*
- **Independent first**: usually no comma
  - *I called **when I arrived**.*
