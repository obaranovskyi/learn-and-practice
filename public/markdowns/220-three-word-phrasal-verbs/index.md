# Three-Word Phrasal Verbs

Three-word phrasal verbs have a verb plus two particles. They are always inseparable.

## Structure

verb + adverb + preposition + object

## Common Three-Word Phrasal Verbs

- **look forward to**: anticipate → *I look forward to seeing you.*
- **put up with**: tolerate → *I can't put up with this noise.*
- **get along with**: have good relationship → *She gets along with everyone.*
- **catch up with**: reach same level → *I need to catch up with the class.*
- **come up with**: think of/invent → *He came up with a great idea.*
- **run out of**: have no more → *We ran out of milk.*
- **keep up with**: maintain pace → *I can't keep up with the changes.*
- **look up to**: respect/admire → *Kids look up to athletes.*
- **look down on**: consider inferior → *Don't look down on others.*
- **get away with**: escape punishment → *He got away with cheating.*
- **face up to**: accept/confront → *Face up to your responsibilities.*
- **make up for**: compensate → *I'll make up for lost time.*

## Note

- Always inseparable: *put up with it* (NOT *put it up with*)
- Gerund after "to": *look forward to seeing* (NOT *to see*)
