# Literal vs Idiomatic Phrasal Verbs

Phrasal verbs can have literal (physical) or idiomatic (figurative) meanings.

## Literal Meanings

The meaning is clear from the words:
- **sit down**: physically sit → *Please sit down.*
- **stand up**: physically stand → *Stand up straight.*
- **pick up**: lift → *Pick up the box.*
- **turn around**: rotate → *Turn around and face me.*
- **go in/out**: enter/exit → *Go in through that door.*

## Idiomatic Meanings

The meaning cannot be guessed from the words:
- **pick up**: learn casually → *She picked up Spanish quickly.*
- **turn around**: improve situation → *The company turned around.*
- **go in**: (of sun) disappear → *The sun went in.*
- **work out**: exercise → *I work out every day.*
- **work out**: succeed → *Things will work out.*

## Same Verb, Different Meanings

**Take off**:
- Literal: Remove → *Take off your coat.*
- Idiomatic: Become successful → *Her career took off.*
- Idiomatic: Leave quickly → *We took off early.*

**Break down**:
- Literal: Destroy → *They broke down the door.*
- Idiomatic: Stop working → *My car broke down.*
- Idiomatic: Cry → *She broke down in tears.*
