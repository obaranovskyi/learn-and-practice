# Irregular Comparisons

Some adjectives have irregular comparative and superlative forms.

## The Irregular Forms

| Adjective | Comparative | Superlative |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| good | better | best |
| bad | worse | worst |
| far | farther/further | farthest/furthest |
| little | less | least |
| much/many | more | most |
| old | older/elder | oldest/eldest |

## Good / Better / Best

- *This is a **good** book.*
- *This book is **better** than that one.*
- *This is the **best** book I've read.*

## Bad / Worse / Worst

- *The weather is **bad** today.*
- *Yesterday was **worse**.*
- *Monday was the **worst**.*

## Far / Farther vs Further

- **farther/farthest**: physical distance
  - *London is farther than Paris.*
- **further/furthest**: abstract/additional
  - *Further information is available.*
  - *Let's discuss this further.*

## Old / Older vs Elder

- **older**: general use
  - *She's older than me.*
- **elder**: family members (attributive only)
  - *my elder brother* (âœ— He is elder)
