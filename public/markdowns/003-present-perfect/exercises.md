# Exercises: Present Perfect Simple

## Exercise 1: Fill in the Blanks
Complete with Present Perfect.

1. I ______ (never/be) to Australia.
2. She ______ (just/finish) her homework.
3. ______ you ______ (see) the new movie?
4. They ______ (live) here since 2010.
5. He ______ (not/call) me yet.

**Answers:**
1. have never been
2. has just finished
3. Have, seen
4. have lived
5. hasn't called

## Exercise 2: For or Since?

1. I've known her ______ three years.
2. He's worked here ______ January.
3. We haven't met ______ ages.
4. She's been sick ______ Monday.
5. They've lived abroad ______ a long time.

**Answers:**
1. for
2. since
3. for
4. since
5. for

## Exercise 3: Present Perfect or Simple Past?

1. I ______ (see) that movie last week.
2. She ______ (never/eat) Mexican food.
3. We ______ (go) to the beach yesterday.
4. ______ you ______ (finish) your work yet?
5. He ______ (live) in London from 2010 to 2015.

**Answers:**
1. saw (finished time - last week)
2. has never eaten (experience)
3. went (finished time - yesterday)
4. Have, finished (unfinished time)
5. lived (finished period)

## Exercise 4: Put the Words in Order

1. just / finished / I / have / breakfast
2. she / yet / hasn't / arrived
3. ever / you / been / have / to Japan / ?
4. already / they / left / have
5. met / we / since / haven't / June

**Answers:**
1. I have just finished breakfast.
2. She hasn't arrived yet.
3. Have you ever been to Japan?
4. They have already left.
5. We haven't met since June.

## Exercise 5: Correct the Mistakes

1. I have went to the store.
2. She has seen him yesterday.
3. Have you ever visit Paris?
4. I know him since 2010.
5. He has already ate lunch.

**Answers:**
1. I have **gone** to the store.
2. She **saw** him yesterday.
3. Have you ever **visited** Paris?
4. I **have known** him since 2010.
5. He has already **eaten** lunch.
