# Present Perfect Simple

Connects the past to the present. Form: **have/has + past participle**

## Uses

### 1. Experiences (ever/never)
- I **have visited** Paris.
- **Have** you **ever** been to Japan?

### 2. Recent Actions with Results
- I **have lost** my keys. (don't have them now)
- She **has broken** her leg.

### 3. Unfinished Time Periods
- I **have seen** two movies this week.

### 4. For/Since (duration)
- I **have lived** here **for** 10 years.
- She **has worked** here **since** 2015.

## For vs Since

| FOR (duration) | SINCE (starting point) |
|----------------|------------------------|
| for two hours | since 2 o'clock |
| for five years | since 2019 |

## Just, Already, Yet

- I **have just** finished. (very recent)
- She **has already** left. (sooner than expected)
- Have you finished **yet**? (questions/negatives)

## vs Simple Past

- I've seen him today. ✓ (unfinished time)
- I saw him yesterday. ✓ (finished time)
- ❌ I have seen him yesterday.
