# Collective Nouns

Collective nouns refer to groups and can take singular or plural verbs.

## Common Collective Nouns

- *family, team, class, staff*
- *government, committee, company*
- *audience, crowd, public*
- *army, navy, police*
- *group, band, crew*

## Singular or Plural?

**Singular** (unit acting as one):
- *The team is winning.* (the whole team)
- *The family has decided.* (as a unit)
- *The committee meets weekly.*

**Plural** (individuals acting separately):
- *The team are arguing among themselves.*
- *The family have different opinions.*
- *The staff are taking their breaks.*

## British vs American English

- **BrE**: More likely to use plural
  - *The government are discussing...*
- **AmE**: More likely to use singular
  - *The government is discussing...*

## Always Plural: "Police"

- ✓ *The police are investigating.*
- ✗ *The police is investigating.*
- For one: *a police officer*

## Animal Groups

- *a flock of birds/sheep*
- *a herd of cattle/elephants*
- *a pack of wolves/dogs*
- *a school of fish*
- *a swarm of bees*
