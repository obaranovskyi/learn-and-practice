# Just, Only, Even

These focusing adverbs emphasize specific elements.

## Just

### Meaning 1: Very recently
- *I've **just** arrived.*
- *She **just** called.*

### Meaning 2: Only/merely
- *It's **just** a game.*
- *I **just** want to help.*

### Meaning 3: Exactly
- *That's **just** what I needed!*
- *It's **just** right.*

## Only

**Meaning**: nothing more, limited to
**Position**: before the word it modifies

- ***Only** John came.* (no one else)
- *John **only** came.* (nothing more - just came)
- *John came **only** yesterday.* (as recently as)

Different positions = different meanings:
- ***Only** I love you.* (no one else loves you)
- *I **only** love you.* (I do nothing but love you)
- *I love **only** you.* (you and no one else)

## Even

**Meaning**: surprisingly, including extreme cases
**Position**: before the word it emphasizes

- ***Even** a child could do it.* (surprisingly)
- *She didn't **even** try.*
- *I like **even** spicy food.*

## Common Patterns

- *just about* (almost)
- *only just* (barely)
- *even so* (nevertheless)
- *even though* (despite)
