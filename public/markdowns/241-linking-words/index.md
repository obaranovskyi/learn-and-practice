# Linking Words

Linking words connect ideas and show relationships.

## Contrast
- **However**: *It was raining. However, we went out.*
- **Nevertheless/Nonetheless**: *It was hard. Nevertheless, she succeeded.*
- **On the other hand**: *He's smart. On the other hand, he's lazy.*
- **In contrast**: *She loves cities. In contrast, he prefers the countryside.*

## Result/Consequence
- **Therefore**: *He didn't study. Therefore, he failed.*
- **Consequently/As a result**: *The road was icy. Consequently, there were accidents.*
- **Thus**: *The evidence is clear. Thus, we must act.*
- **Hence**: *He was tired; hence, he slept early.*

## Addition
- **Moreover/Furthermore**: *It's cheap. Moreover, it's effective.*
- **In addition/Additionally**: *She speaks French. In addition, she knows German.*
- **Besides**: *Besides being smart, she's kind.*

## Emphasis
- **Indeed**: *He is talented. Indeed, he's the best.*
- **In fact/Actually**: *I thought it was bad. In fact, it was terrible.*
- **Certainly/Definitely**: *This will certainly help.*

## Position in Sentence
- Beginning: *However, we decided to go.*
- Middle: *We, however, decided to go.*
- End (less common): *We decided to go, however.*

## Punctuation
- Usually: comma after linking word at beginning
- Or: semicolon before, comma after
