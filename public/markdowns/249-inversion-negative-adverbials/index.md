# Inversion After Negative Adverbials

When negative/restrictive expressions start a sentence, subject-verb order inverts.

## Structure

Negative adverb + auxiliary + subject + main verb

## Common Expressions

### Never/Rarely/Seldom
- *Never **have I seen** such beauty.*
- *Rarely **does she** complain.*
- *Seldom **do we** get such weather.*

### Not only... but also
- *Not only **did he** arrive late, but he also forgot the documents.*

### No sooner... than
- *No sooner **had we** arrived than it started raining.*

### Hardly/Scarcely... when
- *Hardly **had I** sat down when the phone rang.*
- *Scarcely **had they** left when...*

### Little
- *Little **did he** know what awaited him.*
- *Little **do they** realize the danger.*

### At no time / On no account / Under no circumstances
- *At no time **was I** informed.*
- *Under no circumstances **should you** open the door.*

### Only
- *Only then **did I** understand.*
- *Only after the meeting **did we** learn the truth.*

## Without Inversion (mid-sentence)

- *I have never seen such beauty.* (normal)
- *Never have I seen such beauty.* (inverted, emphatic)

## Formal/Literary Style

Inversion sounds formal/literary - common in:
- Writing
- Speeches
- Headlines
