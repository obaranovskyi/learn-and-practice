# Reciprocal Pronouns

Reciprocal pronouns show a mutual action between two or more people.

## The Two Reciprocal Pronouns

- **each other** (traditionally for 2 people)
- **one another** (traditionally for 3+ people)

*In modern English, they're often interchangeable.*

## Usage

Show mutual/two-way action:
- *They love **each other**.* (A loves B, B loves A)
- *The students helped **one another**.*
- *We see **each other** every week.*

## Reciprocal vs Reflexive

| Reciprocal | Reflexive |
|------------|-----------|
| *They helped **each other**.* | *They helped **themselves**.* |
| (A helped B, B helped A) | (A helped A, B helped B) |

- *They looked at **each other**.* (mutual gaze)
- *They looked at **themselves**.* (in mirror)

## Possessive Form

- *each other's* / *one another's*
- *They read **each other's** books.*
- *We know **one another's** secrets.*

## Common Phrases

- *get along with each other*
- *talk to each other*
- *understand each other*
- *help one another*
