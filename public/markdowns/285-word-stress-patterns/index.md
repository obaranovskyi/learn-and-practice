# Word Stress Patterns

English words have stressed and unstressed syllables.

## Two-Syllable Nouns & Adjectives

Usually stress on **first** syllable:
- **TA**ble, **WA**ter, **HAP**py, **YEL**low
- **DOC**tor, **STU**dent, **MU**sic

## Two-Syllable Verbs

Often stress on **second** syllable:
- be**GIN**, ar**RIVE**, re**LAX**, de**CIDE**
- a**GREE**, at**TACK**, sup**PORT**

## Same Word, Different Stress

Noun vs Verb:
| Noun (1st) | Verb (2nd) |
|------------|------------|
| **RE**cord | re**CORD** |
| **PRE**sent | pre**SENT** |
| **OB**ject | ob**JECT** |
| **CON**tract | con**TRACT** |
| **PER**mit | per**MIT** |

## Suffix Patterns

### Stress before suffix
- -tion: educa**TION**, informa**TION**
- -sion: deci**SION**, televi**SION**
- -ic: rea**LIS**tic, scien**TIF**ic
- -ical: his**TOR**ical, po**LIT**ical

### Stress on suffix
- -ee: employ**EE**, refu**GEE**
- -eer: engi**NEER**, volun**TEER**
- -ese: Japa**NESE**, Chi**NESE**

## Compound Nouns

Usually stress on **first** word:
- **AIR**port, **BOOK**store, **FOOT**ball
- **HAND**bag, **SUN**glasses

## Why Stress Matters

Wrong stress can cause misunderstanding:
- **DES**ert (sand) vs de**SERT** (leave)
- **PRE**sent (gift) vs pre**SENT** (give)
