# Simple Present Tense

The Simple Present describes habits, general truths, and scheduled events.

## Form

| Subject | Affirmative | Negative | Question |
|---------|-------------|----------|----------|
| I/You/We/They | work | don't work | Do ... work? |
| He/She/It | works | doesn't work | Does ... work? |

## Uses

### 1. Habits and Routines
- I **drink** coffee every morning.
- She **goes** to the gym twice a week.

### 2. General Facts and Truths
- Water **boils** at 100°C.
- The sun **rises** in the east.

### 3. Permanent Situations
- He **lives** in London.
- She **works** for a bank.

### 4. Scheduled Events (Future)
- The train **leaves** at 9 AM.
- The meeting **starts** tomorrow.

## Spelling: Third Person (he/she/it)

| Ending | Rule | Example |
|--------|------|---------|
| Most verbs | +s | work → works |
| -s, -sh, -ch, -x, -o | +es | watch → watches |
| consonant + y | y → ies | study → studies |

## Time Expressions
always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never, every day, on Mondays

## Common Mistakes

❌ He work every day. → ✅ He **works** every day.
❌ She don't like it. → ✅ She **doesn't** like it.
