# Simple Present Tense

The Simple Present is one of the most commonly used tenses in English. It describes habits, general truths, and scheduled events.

## Form

### Affirmative
| Subject | Verb |
|---------|------|
| I / You / We / They | work |
| He / She / It | work**s** |

### Negative
| Subject | Auxiliary | Verb |
|---------|-----------|------|
| I / You / We / They | do not (don't) | work |
| He / She / It | does not (doesn't) | work |

### Question
| Auxiliary | Subject | Verb |
|-----------|---------|------|
| Do | I / you / we / they | work? |
| Does | he / she / it | work? |

## Uses

### 1. Habits and Routines
Actions that happen regularly or repeatedly.

- I **drink** coffee every morning.
- She **goes** to the gym three times a week.
- They **play** tennis on Sundays.

### 2. General Facts and Truths
Things that are always or generally true.

- Water **boils** at 100°C.
- The sun **rises** in the east.
- Cats **like** fish.

### 3. Permanent Situations
States that are generally permanent.

- He **lives** in London.
- She **works** for a bank.
- I **speak** three languages.

### 4. Scheduled Events (Future)
Timetables and fixed schedules.

- The train **leaves** at 9:00 AM.
- The meeting **starts** in an hour.
- The shop **opens** at 8 AM tomorrow.

### 5. Instructions and Directions
- First, you **turn** left, then you **go** straight.
- You **press** the button and **wait**.

## Spelling Rules for Third Person (he/she/it)

| Verb ending | Rule | Example |
|-------------|------|---------|
| Most verbs | Add **-s** | work → works |
| -s, -sh, -ch, -x, -o | Add **-es** | watch → watches |
| Consonant + y | Change y to **-ies** | study → studies |
| Vowel + y | Add **-s** | play → plays |

## Time Expressions

Common time expressions used with Simple Present:
- always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never
- every day/week/month/year
- on Mondays, in the morning
- once/twice a week

## Common Mistakes

❌ He work every day.  
✅ He **works** every day.

❌ She don't like coffee.  
✅ She **doesn't** like coffee.

❌ Does she works here?  
✅ Does she **work** here?
