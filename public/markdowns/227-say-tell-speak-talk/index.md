# Say vs Tell vs Speak vs Talk

## SAY

Focus on **the words/message**

- say + words: *He said "hello."*
- say + that-clause: *She said that she was tired.*
- say to someone: *He said to me that...*
- ‚ùå say me (NO direct object)

### Fixed expressions
- say yes/no, say sorry, say thank you
- say hello/goodbye, say a word
- say a prayer, say your name

## TELL

Focus on **giving information to someone**

- tell + person: *Tell me the truth.*
- tell someone + that: *She told me that she was leaving.*
- tell someone + to do: *He told me to wait.*

### Fixed expressions
- tell a story/joke/lie
- tell the truth, tell a secret
- tell the time, tell the difference

## SPEAK

Focus on **the act of speaking** (more formal)

- speak + language: *She speaks French.*
- speak to/with someone: *May I speak to Mr. Smith?*
- speak about/of: *He spoke about his plans.*

### Fixed expressions
- speak your mind, speak up
- generally speaking, so to speak

## TALK

Focus on **conversation** (more informal)

- talk to/with someone: *We talked for hours.*
- talk about: *Let's talk about the plan.*
- talk + adjective: *talk loudly*

### Fixed expressions
- small talk, talk nonsense/sense
- talk business, talk shop
