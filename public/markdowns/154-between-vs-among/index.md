# Between vs Among

## Between

**Traditional rule**: two things
**Modern use**: also for distinct individuals

- ***Between** you and me...* (two people)
- *The house is **between** the park and the school.*
- *Choose **between** the red and blue.*
- *The money was divided **between** the three brothers.* (individuals)
- *Relations **between** the countries are good.*

**Used when**: items are distinct/separate

## Among

**Use**: more than two, part of a group

- *She sat **among** her friends.*
- *He's popular **among** students.*
- *The book was hidden **among** others.*
- *It's common **among** teenagers.*

**Used when**: items are not distinct, or surrounded by

## Key Difference

| Between | Among |
|---------|-------|
| distinct items | group/mass |
| specific relationships | surrounded by |
| *between A and B* | *among the crowd* |

## Examples

- *The secret stays **between** us three.* (specific people)
- *There was disagreement **among** the members.* (within group)
- *Trade **between** Japan, China, and Korea* (specific countries)
- *Popular **among** young people* (within demographic)

## Amongst

- **amongst** = formal/literary variant of **among**
- More common in British English
