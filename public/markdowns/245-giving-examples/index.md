# Giving Examples

Words and phrases to introduce examples.

## Introducing Examples

- **For example**: *Many countries, for example France and Spain, use the euro.*
- **For instance**: *Some fruits, for instance apples and oranges, are high in vitamin C.*
- **Such as**: *Citrus fruits such as lemons and oranges...*
- **Like**: *Fruits like apples and oranges...* (informal)
- **e.g.** (exempli gratia): *Citrus fruits (e.g., lemons, oranges)*

## Sentence Position

### Beginning
- *For example, some people prefer tea.*
- *For instance, consider the case of...*

### Middle (parenthetical)
- *Some cities, for example Paris, are very expensive.*
- *Fruits such as apples contain vitamins.*

### End
- *Many European countries use it—France, for example.*

## Such as vs Like

- **Such as**: more formal, introduces examples
- **Like**: informal, also means "similar to"

## Including vs Such as

- **Such as**: implies partial list
  - *Fruits such as apples...* (apples are an example)
- **Including**: implies list is part of larger whole
  - *Many items, including food...* (food is included)

## Punctuation

- **For example/For instance**: comma after
- **Such as**: no comma before
- **e.g.**: comma after, period after each letter

## Common Mistakes
- ❌ *For example like apples...* (redundant)
- ❌ *Such as for example...* (redundant)
