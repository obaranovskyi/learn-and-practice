# Nominal That-Clauses

That-clauses function as nouns (subjects, objects, complements).

## As Subject

- ***That he lied** is obvious.*
- ***That she passed** surprised everyone.*
- ***That they're coming** is good news.*

Note: Often use "It" + extraposition (see topic 252):
- *It is obvious **that he lied**.*

## As Object

After verbs like think, know, believe, say:
- *I think **that she's right**.*
- *He said **that he would come**.*
- *She knows **that it's true**.*

"That" often omitted:
- *I think she's right.* (informal)

## As Complement

After "be":
- *The problem is **that we have no money**.*
- *The truth is **that he lied**.*
- *My concern is **that it's too late**.*

## After Nouns

- *The fact **that she resigned** shocked us.*
- *The idea **that he would help** was naive.*
- *The news **that they won** spread quickly.*

## After Adjectives

- *I'm glad **that you came**.*
- *She's certain **that it will work**.*
- *He's aware **that there's a problem**.*

## Subjunctive in That-Clauses

After suggest, demand, recommend:
- *I suggest **that he be** more careful.*
- *She demanded **that he leave**.*
