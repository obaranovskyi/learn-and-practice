# Comparative and Superlative Adverbs

Adverbs can be compared like adjectives.

## Short Adverbs (-er/-est)

Same form as adjective:
- *fast → faster → fastest*
- *hard → harder → hardest*
- *early → earlier → earliest*
- *late → later → latest*

*He runs **faster** than me.*
*She works the **hardest**.*

## Long Adverbs (more/most)

Adverbs ending in -ly:
- *quickly → more quickly → most quickly*
- *carefully → more carefully → most carefully*
- *beautifully → more beautifully → most beautifully*

*She speaks **more clearly** than him.*
*He drives the **most carefully**.*

## Irregular Adverbs

| Adverb | Comparative | Superlative |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| well | better | best |
| badly | worse | worst |
| far | farther/further | farthest/furthest |
| little | less | least |
| much | more | most |

## Structure

**Comparative**: adverb + than
- *She arrived **earlier than** expected.*

**Superlative**: the + superlative
- *He performed **the best** of all.*

## Notes

- "the" is often optional with superlative adverbs
- *Who runs (the) fastest?*
