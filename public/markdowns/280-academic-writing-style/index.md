# Academic Writing Style

Conventions for essays, research papers, and scholarly work.

## Key Features

### Formal vocabulary
- *examine* not *look at*
- *demonstrate* not *show*
- *utilize* not *use*
- *obtain* not *get*

### Objectivity
- Avoid personal opinions (usually)
- Use hedging: "It could be argued that..."
- Passive voice common: "The study was conducted..."

### Precision
- Specific, accurate language
- Define key terms
- Avoid vague words (stuff, things)

## Structure Words

### Introducing
- *This essay will examine...*
- *The purpose of this paper is...*

### Adding points
- *Furthermore, Moreover, Additionally*
- *In addition, What is more*

### Contrasting
- *However, Nevertheless, On the other hand*
- *Although, Despite, In contrast*

### Concluding
- *In conclusion, To summarize*
- *The evidence suggests that...*

## Avoid in Academic Writing

- Contractions (don't, can't)
- First person (sometimes): I think â†’ It could be argued
- Colloquialisms/slang
- Rhetorical questions (usually)
- Exclamation marks
- Informal discourse markers (so, well, anyway)

## Hedging Language

- *It appears that... / It seems that...*
- *This suggests... / This indicates...*
- *It is possible that... / It may be that...*
- *The evidence tends to suggest...*

## Citation Phrases

- *According to Smith (2020),...*
- *As Jones argues,...*
- *Research has shown that...*
