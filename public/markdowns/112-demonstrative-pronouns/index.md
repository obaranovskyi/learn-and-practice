# Demonstrative Pronouns

Demonstrative pronouns point to specific things.

## The Four Demonstratives

|  | Near | Far |
|--|------|-----|
| Singular | this | that |
| Plural | these | those |

## Near vs Far

### Near (this/these)
- *What's **this**?* (holding something)
- ***This** is my book.*
- ***These** are my keys.*

### Far (that/those)
- *What's **that** over there?*
- ***That** is a beautiful sunset.*
- ***Those** are expensive.*

## Time References

- **This/these**: present, current, upcoming
  - *this week, this morning, these days*
- **That/those**: past, distant
  - *that day, those years, at that time*

## As Pronouns vs Adjectives

| Pronoun (alone) | Adjective (+ noun) |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| *This is nice.* | *This book is nice.* |
| *I want those.* | *I want those shoes.* |

## Telephone Introductions

- *Hello, **this** is John speaking.* (introducing yourself)
- *Is **that** Mary?* (asking who's calling)

## Common Expressions

- *That's right/true*
- *This is it!*
- *That's all*
- *Like this / Like that*
