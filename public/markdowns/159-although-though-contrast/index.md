# Although, Though, Even though (Contrast)

These conjunctions show contrast or concession.

## Although / Though

**Meaning**: despite the fact that
- ***Although** it was raining, we went out.*
- ***Though** she's young, she's very wise.*
- *I enjoyed it, **although/though** it was long.*

**Though** is slightly less formal.

## Even though

**Stronger** contrast; more surprising
- ***Even though** he studied hard, he failed.*
- *She smiled **even though** she was sad.*
- ***Even though** I'm tired, I'll help you.*

## Though at End (Adverb)

- *I like it. It's expensive, **though**.*
- *The food was good. The service was slow, **though**.*

Only **though** can be used this way (not although).

## While / Whereas

**Meaning**: comparison of differences
- ***While** I like coffee, my wife prefers tea.*
- *He's tall, **whereas** his brother is short.*

## Comparison

| Word | Strength | End position? |
|------|----------|---------------|
| although | medium | no |
| though | medium | yes |
| even though | strong | no |

## Despite / In spite of + Noun

- ***Despite** the rain, we went out.*
- ***In spite of** being tired, she continued.*
- (Not: ~~Despite it was raining~~)
