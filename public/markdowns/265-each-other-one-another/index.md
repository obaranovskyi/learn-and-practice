# Each Other vs One Another

Reciprocal pronouns showing mutual action.

## Traditional Rule

- **Each other**: two people
- **One another**: more than two people

## Modern Usage

In practice, they're **interchangeable**:
- *They love **each other**.* ✓
- *They love **one another**.* ✓

Both are correct for any number.

## Meaning

Both express mutual/reciprocal action:
- *John and Mary love **each other**.*
  = John loves Mary AND Mary loves John

## Possessive Forms

- **Each other's**: *They read each other's books.*
- **One another's**: *They visited one another's homes.*

## Usage Examples

### Two People
- *The couple looked at **each other**.*
- *The two friends helped **each other**.*

### More Than Two
- *The team members supported **one another**.*
- *The students helped **each other** study.*

## vs Reflexive Pronouns

- **Reciprocal** (mutual): *They looked at **each other**.* (A looked at B, B looked at A)
- **Reflexive** (same person): *They looked at **themselves**.* (A looked at A, B looked at B)

## Common Patterns

- help each other
- know each other
- love each other
- talk to each other
- look at one another
- support one another
