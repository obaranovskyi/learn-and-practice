# Prepositions of Movement

Prepositions of movement show direction of motion.

## Common Movement Prepositions

| Preposition | Meaning | Example |
|-------------|---------|---------|
| to | toward destination | Go **to** school. |
| into | entering (inside) | Walk **into** the room. |
| out of | exiting | Get **out of** the car. |
| onto | moving to surface | Jump **onto** the bed. |
| off | leaving surface | Get **off** the bus. |
| through | from one side to other | Walk **through** the tunnel. |
| across | from one side to other (surface) | Swim **across** the river. |
| along | following a line | Walk **along** the beach. |
| past | going by | Drive **past** the church. |
| toward(s) | in direction of | Walk **toward** the exit. |
| up | moving higher | Climb **up** the stairs. |
| down | moving lower | Go **down** the hill. |
| around | in a circle | Walk **around** the lake. |
| over | above and across | Jump **over** the fence. |

## Into vs In

- **into** = movement: *She walked **into** the room.*
- **in** = position: *She's **in** the room.*

## To vs At

- **to** = direction: *Go **to** the door.*
- **at** = position: *Wait **at** the door.*
