# Compound Sentences

A compound sentence has **two or more independent clauses** joined by a coordinator.

## Structure

**Independent Clause + Coordinator + Independent Clause**

## Ways to Join

### 1. Coordinating Conjunctions (FANBOYS)
- *I like tea, **and** she likes coffee.*
- *It rained, **but** we went out.*
- *Study hard, **or** you'll fail.*
- *He was tired, **so** he slept.*

### 2. Semicolon
- *I came; I saw; I conquered.*
- *She's smart; she'll figure it out.*

### 3. Semicolon + Conjunctive Adverb
- *It rained; **however**, we went out.*
- *She studied; **therefore**, she passed.*

## Punctuation Rules

| Method | Punctuation |
|--------|-------------|
| FANBOYS | comma + conjunction |
| Semicolon alone | ; |
| Semicolon + adverb | ; adverb, |

## Each Clause Must Be Independent

Both clauses could stand alone as sentences:
- *I like tea.* ✓ (independent)
- *She likes coffee.* ✓ (independent)
- *I like tea, and she likes coffee.* ✓ (compound)

## Common Conjunctive Adverbs

- *however, therefore, moreover*
- *furthermore, nevertheless, consequently*
- *meanwhile, otherwise, instead*
