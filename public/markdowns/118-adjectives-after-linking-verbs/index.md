# Adjectives After Linking Verbs

Linking verbs connect the subject to an adjective (not an adverb).

## Common Linking Verbs

### State of Being
- **be**: *She is happy.*
- **seem**: *He seems tired.*
- **appear**: *It appears difficult.*

### Becoming/Changing
- **become**: *She became famous.*
- **get**: *I'm getting hungry.*
- **turn**: *The leaves turned red.*
- **go**: *The milk went bad.*
- **grow**: *He grew impatient.*

### Senses
- **look**: *You look great!*
- **sound**: *That sounds interesting.*
- **smell**: *The food smells delicious.*
- **taste**: *This tastes good.*
- **feel**: *I feel sick.*

## Adjective, Not Adverb!

- ✓ *The soup tastes **good**.* (adjective)
- ✗ *The soup tastes **well**.* (adverb - incorrect)

- ✓ *She looks **beautiful**.* (adjective)
- ✗ *She looks **beautifully**.* (adverb - incorrect)

## Linking vs Action Verbs

Some verbs can be both:

| Linking (+ adjective) | Action (+ adverb) |
|----------------------|-------------------|
| *He looked angry.* | *He looked angrily at me.* |
| *She felt sad.* | *She felt the fabric gently.* |
| *The flower smells sweet.* | *The dog smells carefully.* |
