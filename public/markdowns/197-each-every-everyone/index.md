# Each, Every, Everyone (Singular Agreement)

These words are grammatically **singular** despite referring to multiple items.

## Each

Emphasizes **individuals separately**:
- ***Each** student **has** a book.*
- ***Each** of the students **has** a book.*
- ***Each one is** responsible.*

## Every

Emphasizes **all members of a group**:
- ***Every** student **is** here.*
- ***Every** person **needs** love.*
- ***Every** day **brings** new challenges.*

## Everyone/Everybody

Always singular verb:
- ***Everyone is** welcome.*
- ***Everybody knows** the answer.*
- ***Everyone has** arrived.*

## Everything

Singular:
- ***Everything is** ready.*
- ***Everything was** perfect.*

## Each vs Every

| Each | Every |
|------|-------|
| Individual focus | Group focus |
| Can use "of" | Cannot use "of" |
| *Each of them* ✓ | *Every of them* ✗ |
| 2+ items | 3+ items (usually) |

## Pronoun Reference

Traditional: *Everyone should bring **his or her** book.*
Modern: *Everyone should bring **their** book.* (widely accepted)

## Common Mistakes

- ✗ *Each of them are here.*
- ✓ *Each of them **is** here.*

- ✗ *Everyone have finished.*
- ✓ *Everyone **has** finished.*
