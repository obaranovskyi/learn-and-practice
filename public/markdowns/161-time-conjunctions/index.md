# Time Conjunctions

Time conjunctions show when things happen in relation to each other.

## When / Whenever

- ***When** I arrive, I'll call you.*
- ***Whenever** I see him, he's smiling.* (every time)

## While / As

**During the same time**:
- ***While** I was cooking, he arrived.*
- ***As** we were talking, she walked in.*

## Before / After

- *Call me **before** you leave.*
- ***After** I finish, I'll help you.*
- *He arrived **after** I had left.*

## Until / Till

**Up to a point in time**:
- *Wait **until** I come back.*
- *We worked **till** midnight.*

## As soon as

**Immediately when**:
- ***As soon as** I arrive, I'll call.*
- *Call me **as soon as** you can.*

## Once

**After (and because)**:
- ***Once** you understand, it's easy.*
- ***Once** I finish this, I'll help.*

## Present Tense for Future

In time clauses about the future, use **present** tense:
- ✓ ***When** I **arrive**, I'll call.* (not: will arrive)
- ✓ ***Before** she **leaves**, say goodbye.* (not: will leave)
- ✓ ***As soon as** it **stops** raining...* (not: will stop)
