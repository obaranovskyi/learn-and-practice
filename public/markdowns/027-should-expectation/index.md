# Should / Ought to (Expectation)

## Expressing What We Expect

### Should/Ought to (~80% expectation)
- The train **should** arrive at 6. (expected)
- She **ought to** be here soon. (expected)

### Negative
- It **shouldn't** take long.
- There **shouldn't** be any problems.

## vs Must (Deduction)
- **Should** = expected (based on normal circumstances)
- **Must** = certain (based on logic/evidence)

"She's studying hard. She **should** pass." (expected)
"She got 100%. She **must** have passed." (certain)
