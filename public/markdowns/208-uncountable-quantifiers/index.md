# Uncountable Quantifiers

These quantifiers are used **only with uncountable nouns**.

## Much

Large amount (mainly negatives/questions):
- *Is there **much** traffic?*
- *I don't have **much** time.*
- *How **much** money do you need?*

## Little / A little

Small amount:
- ***A little** patience helps.* (some, positive)
- *There's **little** hope.* (not much, negative)
- *Very **little** information is available.*

## An amount of

Singular verb:
- ***An amount of** money **was** donated.*

## A great deal of (formal)

Large amount:
- ***A great deal of** work is required.*
- *She has **a great deal of** experience.*

## Common Uncountable Quantifiers

| Quantifier | Meaning |
|------------|---------|
| much | large amount |
| little | small amount (negative) |
| a little | small amount (positive) |
| a bit of | small amount (informal) |
| a great deal of | large amount (formal) |
| an amount of | some (singular verb) |

## Remember

These ONLY work with uncountable nouns:
- ✓ *much water, little information, a bit of advice*
- ✗ *much books, little friends, a bit of cars*

For countable, use: *many, few, a few, several*
