# Borrow vs Lend

Opposite directions of temporary transfer.

## Borrow = Receive/Take temporarily

You receive something from someone:
- *Can I **borrow** your pen?* (take from you)
- *I **borrowed** money from the bank.*
- *She **borrowed** my book.*

Structure: borrow (something) from (someone)

## Lend = Give temporarily

You give something to someone:
- *Can you **lend** me your pen?* (give to me)
- *The bank **lent** me money.*
- *I'll **lend** you my book.*

Structure: lend (something) to (someone) OR lend (someone) (something)

## Direction

Think about who has the item:
- **Borrow**: item comes TO you
- **Lend**: item goes FROM you

## Common Patterns

| Asking to receive | Asking to give |
|-------------------|----------------|
| Can I borrow...? | Can you lend me...? |
| I borrowed it from him | He lent it to me |

## Memory Trick

- **B**orrow = **B**ring to me
- **L**end = **L**eave with someone

## Common Mistakes

- ❌ *Can you borrow me some money?*
- ✅ *Can you lend me some money?*
- ✅ *Can I borrow some money from you?*

## Similar: Rent vs Let (UK)

- **Rent**: pay to use (tenant)
- **Let**: receive payment for use (landlord)
