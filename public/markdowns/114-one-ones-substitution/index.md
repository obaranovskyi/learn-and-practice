# One / Ones (Substitution)

**One** and **ones** replace nouns to avoid repetition.

## Basic Usage

### One (singular)
- *I need a pen. Can I borrow **one**?*
- *Which shirt? The blue **one**.*
- *This book is better than that **one**.*

### Ones (plural)
- *I like these shoes, but not those **ones**.*
- *Which apples? The red **ones**.*
- *These photos are better than the old **ones**.*

## With Adjectives

- *I don't want the big one. I want the small **one**.*
- *The new computers are faster than the old **ones**.*

## With "This/That/Which"

- ***This one** or **that one**?*
- ***Which one** do you want?*
- ***These ones** are mine.* (informal)
- ***Those ones** are yours.* (informal)

## When NOT to Use "One"

Don't use after:
- Possessives: *yours* (not: your one)
- *Both, neither, each, either*
- After numbers without adjectives: *I'll take two* (not: two ones)

## One as Impersonal Pronoun

Formal/general statements:
- ***One** should always be polite.*
- ***One** never knows what might happen.*
(Informal: *You never know...*)
