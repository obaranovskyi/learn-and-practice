# Nouns That Are Always Plural

Some nouns only exist in plural form and take plural verbs.

## Paired Items (Two Parts)

- *scissors* - Where are the scissors?
- *glasses/spectacles* - My glasses are broken.
- *trousers/pants* - These trousers are new.
- *jeans* - Your jeans look great.
- *shorts* - The shorts are too big.
- *pajamas* - My pajamas are comfortable.
- *binoculars* - The binoculars were expensive.
- *pliers* - These pliers are useful.

**To count**: Use "a pair of"
- *a pair of scissors*
- *two pairs of jeans*

## Other Always-Plural Nouns

- *clothes* - Her clothes are fashionable.
- *goods* - The goods have arrived.
- *belongings* - His belongings were stolen.
- *earnings* - My earnings are sufficient.
- *savings* - Our savings are in the bank.
- *surroundings* - The surroundings are beautiful.
- *remains* - The remains were found.
- *thanks* - Thanks are in order.
- *congratulations* - Congratulations are due!

## Usage Notes

- ✓ *The scissors are on the table.*
- ✗ *The scissor is on the table.*
- ✓ *These jeans fit well.*
- ✗ *This jean fits well.*
