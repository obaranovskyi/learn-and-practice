# Adverbs of Degree

Adverbs of degree modify adjectives, adverbs, or verbs to show **intensity**.

## Common Adverbs of Degree

| Adverb | Intensity |
|--------|-----------|
| extremely, incredibly | very high |
| very, really, quite | high |
| fairly, rather, pretty | moderate |
| slightly, a bit, a little | low |
| hardly, barely, scarcely | almost none |

## Position

### Before adjectives/adverbs
- *She's **very** tall.*
- *He speaks **quite** quickly.*
- *It's **extremely** hot.*

### With verbs
- *I **really** like it.*
- *She **almost** finished.*
- *They **completely** forgot.*

## Quite (Two Meanings)

- With gradable: **fairly** - *quite good* (= fairly good)
- With non-gradable: **completely** - *quite perfect* (= absolutely)

## Enough

Position: **after** adjectives/adverbs
- *It's warm **enough**.* (not: enough warm)
- *She runs fast **enough**.*
- *He's old **enough** to vote.*

## Very vs Too

- **very** = high degree (neutral)
- **too** = excessive (problem)

*It's **very** hot.* (observation)
*It's **too** hot to go out.* (problem)
