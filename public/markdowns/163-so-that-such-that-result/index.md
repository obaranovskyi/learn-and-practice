# So...that, Such...that (Result)

These structures show a result caused by something.

## So + Adjective/Adverb + That

- *It was **so cold that** we stayed inside.*
- *She spoke **so quickly that** I couldn't understand.*
- *He's **so tall that** he hits his head on doors.*
- *They worked **so hard that** they finished early.*

## Such + (Adjective) + Noun + That

- *It was **such a cold day that** we stayed in.*
- *She's **such a good singer that** everyone loves her.*
- *He told **such funny jokes that** we laughed all night.*
- *It was **such bad weather that** we cancelled.*

## So vs Such

| Structure | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| so + adjective | so cold |
| so + adverb | so quickly |
| such + a/an + adj + noun | such a nice day |
| such + adj + plural noun | such nice people |
| such + adj + uncountable | such good advice |

## So Much/Many/Little/Few

- *There was **so much traffic that** we were late.*
- *She has **so many friends that** she's never alone.*
- *He had **so little time that** he couldn't finish.*
- *There were **so few people that** they cancelled.*

## Without "That"

In informal speech, "that" can be omitted:
- *I'm **so** tired (that) I could sleep anywhere.*
- *It was **such** a long day!*
