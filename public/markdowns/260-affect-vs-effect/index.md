# Affect vs Effect

Usually: **affect** = verb, **effect** = noun.

## Affect (Verb)

To influence or change:
- *The weather **affects** my mood.*
- *This will **affect** your grade.*
- *Stress **affects** health.*
- *How did it **affect** you?*

## Effect (Noun)

A result or outcome:
- *The **effect** was immediate.*
- *What's the **effect** of this drug?*
- *Cause and **effect***
- *Side **effects***

## Memory Trick

- **A**ffect = **A**ction (verb)
- **E**ffect = **E**nd result (noun)

Or: **RAVEN**
- **R**emember: **A**ffect = **V**erb, **E**ffect = **N**oun

## Exceptions

### Effect as Verb (rare, formal)
To bring about:
- *He **effected** change in the company.* (= brought about)

### Affect as Noun (psychology)
Emotional state:
- *The patient showed flat **affect**.* (technical term)

## Examples

| Sentence | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| How will this ___ me? | affect (verb) |
| The ___ was dramatic | effect (noun) |
| It won't ___ the outcome | affect (verb) |
| Negative side ___ | effects (noun) |

## Common Mistakes

- ❌ *This will effect your score.*
- ✅ *This will affect your score.*
- ❌ *The affect was positive.*
- ✅ *The effect was positive.*
