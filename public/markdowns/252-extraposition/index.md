# Extraposition

Moving a clause to the end and using "it" as a placeholder.

## Basic Pattern

- Heavy subject: ***That he passed the exam** surprised us.*
- Extraposed: ***It** surprised us **that he passed the exam**.*

## With Adjectives

- *It's important **that you arrive on time**.*
- *It's obvious **that she's lying**.*
- *It's strange **that he didn't call**.*

Common adjectives: important, clear, obvious, strange, likely, possible, necessary, essential

## With Nouns

- *It's a fact **that the earth is round**.*
- *It's a pity **that you can't come**.*
- *It's no surprise **that she won**.*

## With Infinitives

- *It's easy **to make mistakes**.*
- *It's nice **to meet you**.*
- *It's impossible **to know everything**.*

## With -ing Clauses

- *It's no use **crying over spilt milk**.*
- *It's worth **trying again**.*
- *It's no good **complaining**.*

## Why Use Extraposition?

1. **End-weight**: Heavy info at end
2. **Easier processing**: Subject "it" is simple
3. **Emphasis**: Focus on the clause content

## Comparison

| Normal | Extraposed |
|--------|------------|
| That he won is amazing. | It's amazing that he won. |
| To learn Chinese is hard. | It's hard to learn Chinese. |
