# Emphatic Structures

Ways to add emphasis in English.

## Cleft Sentences (It is/was... that/who)

Focus on specific element:
- Normal: *John broke the window.*
- Emphasis on John: ***It was John** who broke the window.*
- Emphasis on window: ***It was the window** that John broke.*
- Emphasis on time: ***It was yesterday** that he arrived.*

## What-Clefts (Pseudo-clefts)

- ***What I need** is a vacation.*
- ***What she said** was surprising.*
- ***What happened** was unexpected.*
- ***What I like about him** is his honesty.*

## All-Clefts

- ***All I want** is peace.*
- ***All you need** is love.*
- ***All I did** was ask a question.*

## Emphatic Do/Does/Did

- *I **do** understand your concern.*
- *She **does** work hard.*
- *He **did** finish the project.*

## Reflexive Pronouns for Emphasis

- *I **myself** saw it happen.*
- *The president **himself** called.*
- *She made it **herself**.*

## Word Order for Emphasis

Fronting (moving element to beginning):
- *This book I really enjoyed.* (object fronted)
- *Stupid he is not.* (complement fronted)

## Own for Emphasis

- *It's my **own** fault.*
- *I saw it with my **own** eyes.*
