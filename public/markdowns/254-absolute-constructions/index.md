# Absolute Constructions

A phrase with its own subject, grammatically independent from the main clause.

## Structure

Subject + participle (or other element)

## With Present Participle

- ***Weather permitting**, we'll have a picnic.*
  = If the weather permits...
- ***The sun having set**, we went home.*
  = After the sun had set...
- ***All things considered**, it was a success.*

## With Past Participle

- ***The work done**, they went home.*
  = When/After the work was done...
- ***His speech finished**, everyone applauded.*
- ***The problem solved**, we moved on.*

## With Adjective/Noun/Preposition

- ***Knife in hand**, he approached.*
- ***Head high**, she walked away.*
- ***Arms folded**, he waited.*

## Common Fixed Expressions

- *All things considered...*
- *Weather permitting...*
- *God willing...*
- *Other things being equal...*
- *Present company excepted...*

## Purpose

1. **Concise**: Avoids subordinate clauses
2. **Literary/Formal**: More elegant style
3. **Shows relationship**: Time, condition, manner

## Comparison

| Regular Clause | Absolute Construction |
|----------------|----------------------|
| When the sun had set, we left. | The sun having set, we left. |
| If weather permits, we'll go. | Weather permitting, we'll go. |
| After dinner was finished... | Dinner finished, we talked. |
