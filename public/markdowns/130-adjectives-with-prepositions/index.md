# Adjectives with Prepositions

Many adjectives are followed by specific prepositions.

## With "of"

- *afraid of, aware of, capable of*
- *fond of, full of, jealous of*
- *proud of, scared of, tired of*
- *ashamed of, suspicious of*

*She's **afraid of** spiders.*

## With "about"

- *angry about, anxious about*
- *curious about, excited about*
- *happy about, sad about*
- *worried about, nervous about*

*I'm **worried about** the exam.*

## With "at"

- *good at, bad at, brilliant at*
- *angry at, surprised at*
- *skilled at, hopeless at*

*He's **good at** math.*

## With "in"

- *interested in, involved in*
- *successful in, disappointed in*

*She's **interested in** art.*

## With "with"

- *bored with, pleased with*
- *satisfied with, disappointed with*
- *angry with (person), fed up with*
- *familiar with, patient with*

*I'm **bored with** this game.*

## With "for"

- *famous for, responsible for*
- *ready for, sorry for*
- *good for, bad for*

*He's **famous for** his cooking.*

## With "to"

- *similar to, different from/to*
- *married to, engaged to*
- *kind to, rude to, nice to*

*This is **similar to** that.*
