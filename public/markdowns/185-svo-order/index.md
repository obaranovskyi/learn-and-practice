# Subject-Verb-Object (SVO) Order

English follows **SVO** word order in statements.

## Basic Pattern

**Subject + Verb + Object**

- ***She** (S) **reads** (V) **books** (O).*
- ***They** (S) **love** (V) **music** (O).*
- ***I** (S) **eat** (V) **breakfast** (O).*

## Extended Patterns

### S + V (intransitive)
- *Birds **fly**.*
- *She **laughed**.*

### S + V + O (transitive)
- *I **read** books.*
- *She **loves** coffee.*

### S + V + O + O (ditransitive)
- *She **gave** me a gift.*
- *He **told** her the truth.*

### S + V + O + C (object complement)
- *They **made** her happy.*
- *We **elected** him president.*

## Adverb Position

Adverbs can appear in various positions:
- **Front**: *Yesterday, I saw her.*
- **Mid**: *I often see her.*
- **End**: *I saw her yesterday.*

## Common Order

**Subject + Verb + Object + Manner + Place + Time**

- *She played (V) tennis (O) well (M) at the club (P) yesterday (T).*

## Questions Change Order

Statements (SVO) → Questions (VSO or Aux-S-V):
- *She is happy.* → *Is she happy?*
- *You like tea.* → *Do you like tea?*
