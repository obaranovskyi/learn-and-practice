# Relative Pronouns

Relative pronouns introduce relative clauses and refer back to a noun.

## The Relative Pronouns

| Pronoun | Use |
|---------|-----|
| who | people (subject) |
| whom | people (object) - formal |
| whose | possession (people/things) |
| which | things/animals |
| that | people or things (defining) |

## Examples

### Who (subject - people)
*The woman **who** called is my sister.*

### Whom (object - people, formal)
*The person **whom** I met was kind.*
*(Informal: The person **who/that** I met...)*

### Whose (possession)
*The man **whose** car was stolen reported it.*
*The house **whose** roof is red is mine.*

### Which (things)
*The book **which** I bought is interesting.*
*The cat **which** lives here is friendly.*

### That (people or things - defining)
*The book **that** you recommended was great.*
*The man **that** helped me was kind.*

## Defining vs Non-defining

- **Defining**: essential info, no commas, can use *that*
- **Non-defining**: extra info, commas, use *who/which*

*My brother **who** lives in Paris...* (I have several brothers)
*My brother, **who** lives in Paris,...* (I have one brother)
