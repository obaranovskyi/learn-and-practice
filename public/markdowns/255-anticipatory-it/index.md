# Anticipatory It

"It" as a placeholder for clauses that come later.

## As Subject (Extraposition)

Delays heavy subjects:
- ***It** is clear **that he is lying**.*
- ***It** seems **that she has left**.*
- ***It** takes time **to learn a language**.*

Common patterns:
- It + be + adjective + that/to
- It + seems/appears + that
- It + takes + time + to

## As Object

After certain verbs:
- *I find **it** hard **to concentrate**.*
- *She made **it** clear **that she disagreed**.*
- *He considers **it** important **to be punctual**.*

Pattern: verb + it + adjective/noun + clause

Common verbs: find, make, consider, think, believe

## Fixed Expressions with "It"

- *It goes without saying that...*
- *It stands to reason that...*
- *It so happens that...*
- *It turns out that...*
- *It appears/seems that...*

## Different Types of "It"

### Anticipatory (preparatory)
- *It is important to try.* (= To try is important)

### Referential (refers to something)
- *I saw the movie. It was great.*

### Empty/Dummy (no meaning)
- *It's raining.* (weather)
- *It's 3 o'clock.* (time)

## Why Use Anticipatory It?

- End-weight principle (heavy info last)
- Easier to process
- Natural English rhythm
