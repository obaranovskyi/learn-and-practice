# Summarizing

Words and phrases to conclude and summarize.

## Conclusion Words

- **In conclusion**: *In conclusion, the project was successful.*
- **To conclude**: *To conclude, let me summarize the main points.*
- **Finally**: *Finally, I'd like to thank everyone.*
- **Lastly**: *Lastly, remember to save your work.*

## Summary Phrases

- **To sum up**: *To sum up, there are three main issues.*
- **In summary**: *In summary, the results were positive.*
- **To summarize**: *To summarize, we need more funding.*
- **In short**: *In short, it was a success.*
- **In brief**: *In brief, the plan works.*

## Overall Assessment

- **On the whole**: *On the whole, it was a good year.*
- **All in all**: *All in all, we achieved our goals.*
- **Overall**: *Overall, the feedback was positive.*
- **By and large**: *By and large, customers are satisfied.*

## Restating the Main Point

- **In other words**: *In other words, we need to act now.*
- **That is (to say)**: *That is, the project is behind schedule.*
- **Basically**: *Basically, we need more time.*
- **Essentially**: *Essentially, the problem is funding.*

## Formality

**Most formal**: In conclusion, To summarize, In summary
**Neutral**: To sum up, Overall, On the whole
**Less formal**: Basically, In short, All in all

## Structure in Essays

1. Introduction
2. Body paragraphs
3. **Conclusion**: *In conclusion... To sum up... Overall...*
