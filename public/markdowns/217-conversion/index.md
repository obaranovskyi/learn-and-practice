# Conversion (Zero Derivation)

Conversion uses a word as a different part of speech without changing its form.

## Noun → Verb

- *water* (n) → *to water* plants (v)
- *email* (n) → *to email* someone (v)
- *phone* (n) → *to phone* someone (v)
- *bottle* (n) → *to bottle* wine (v)

## Verb → Noun

- *to run* (v) → *a run* (n)
- *to drink* (v) → *a drink* (n)
- *to walk* (v) → *a walk* (n)
- *to try* (v) → *a try* (n)

## Adjective → Verb

- *empty* (adj) → *to empty* (v)
- *clean* (adj) → *to clean* (v)
- *dry* (adj) → *to dry* (v)

## Adjective → Noun

- *the poor, the rich, the young, the old*
- *the impossible, the unknown*

## Modern Examples

Technology creates many conversions:
- *to Google* something
- *to text* someone
- *to message* someone
- *to bookmark* a page
