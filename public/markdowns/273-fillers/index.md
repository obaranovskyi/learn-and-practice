# Fillers

Words used while thinking or organizing thoughts.

## Common Fillers

### Hesitation sounds
- *um...*, *uh...*, *er...*
- (Natural in speech, but overuse = nervousness)

### Planning words
- *well...* - introducing a response/topic
- *so...* - beginning/summarizing
- *right...* - transitioning
- *OK...* - starting new point

### Clarification
- *I mean...* - clarifying previous statement
- *you know...* - assuming shared knowledge
- *like...* - approximating/explaining
- *basically...* - simplifying

### Thinking time
- *let me see...*
- *let me think...*
- *how shall I put it...*

## Functions

### Holding the floor
- *"Well, you see, the thing is..."*
- Signals you're not finished speaking

### Softening
- *"I mean, it's not bad, but..."*
- Makes statements less direct

### Buying time
- *"Um, let me think about that..."*
- Gives time to formulate response

## Appropriate Use

- Natural in conversation
- Overuse can distract listeners
- Reduce in formal presentations
- Write without fillers (usually)

## Cultural Note

Some fillers vary by region:
- American: *like*, *you know*
- British: *sort of*, *kind of*
- All English: *um*, *uh*, *well*
