# Beside vs Besides

These words look similar but have different meanings.

## Beside (no 's')

**Meaning**: next to, at the side of
**Type**: preposition of place

- *Sit **beside** me.* (= next to)
- *The park is **beside** the river.*
- *She stood **beside** her mother.*
- *He placed the book **beside** the lamp.*

**Synonym**: next to, by

## Besides (with 's')

**Meaning 1**: in addition to
- ***Besides** English, she speaks French.* (= as well as)
- ***Besides** being smart, he's kind.*
- *Who else was there **besides** you?*

**Meaning 2**: moreover, furthermore (adverb)
- *I don't want to go. **Besides**, it's too expensive.*
- *It's too late. **Besides**, I'm tired.*

## Comparison

| Beside | Besides |
|--------|---------|
| next to (place) | in addition to |
| *sit beside me* | *besides you, who else?* |
| preposition only | preposition + adverb |

## Memory Tip

- **beside** (short) = position (simple location)
- **besides** (longer) = addition (more meaning)

## Common Mistakes

- ✗ *Besides the river* (meaning: next to)
- ✓ *Beside the river*

- ✗ *Beside English, she speaks French*
- ✓ *Besides English, she speaks French*
