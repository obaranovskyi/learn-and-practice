# Business English Conventions

Professional language for the workplace.

## Common Phrases

### Meetings
- *Let's get started / Let's begin.*
- *Moving on to the next point...*
- *To summarize / In summary...*
- *Does anyone have any questions?*
- *Let's table this for now.* (postpone)

### Emails
- *Just following up on...*
- *As per our discussion...*
- *I wanted to touch base regarding...*
- *Please keep me in the loop.*
- *Let me know if you need anything else.*

### Phone Calls
- *This is [Name] from [Company].*
- *I'm calling regarding...*
- *Could you put me through to...?*
- *I'll get back to you on that.*

## Business Jargon (Common)

- *action items* - tasks
- *bandwidth* - capacity/time
- *circle back* - return to discuss
- *deliverables* - things to produce
- *leverage* - use to advantage
- *stakeholders* - interested parties
- *synergy* - combined effectiveness
- *take it offline* - discuss privately

## Polite Requests

- *Would you mind...?*
- *I was wondering if you could...*
- *When you get a chance, could you...?*

## Softening Bad News

- *Unfortunately,...*
- *I'm afraid that...*
- *Regrettably,...*

## Diplomatic Language

- Direct: "You're wrong."
- Diplomatic: "I see your point, however..."
- Direct: "That won't work."
- Diplomatic: "That's an interesting idea. Have you considered...?"
