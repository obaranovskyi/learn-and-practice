# Either, Neither (Determiners)

For referring to **two** things.

## Either

**One or the other** (of two):
- ***Either** option is fine.*
- *You can take **either** road.*
- ***Either** answer is correct.*

### Either...or
- ***Either** you **or** I will go.*
- *She speaks **either** French **or** German.*

### Either of
- ***Either of** them can help.*
- *I don't like **either of** these.*

## Neither

**Not one and not the other** (of two):
- ***Neither** answer is correct.*
- ***Neither** road leads there.*
- *I want **neither** option.*

### Neither...nor
- ***Neither** she **nor** I knew.*
- *It's **neither** cheap **nor** good.*

### Neither of
- ***Neither of** them came.*
- *I like **neither of** these.*

## As Pronouns

- *Which do you want?* — ***Either** is fine.* / ***Neither**, thanks.*

## Either in Negatives

Meaning "also not":
- *I don't like it.* — *I don't **either**.* (= Neither do I)
- *She can't swim and I can't **either**.*

## Pronunciation

- **Either**: /ˈaɪðər/ or /ˈiːðər/
- **Neither**: /ˈnaɪðər/ or /ˈniːðər/

Both pronunciations are correct.
