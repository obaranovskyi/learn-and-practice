# Prepositions of Time

## At, On, In

| Preposition | Use | Examples |
|-------------|-----|----------|
| **at** | specific times, holidays | at 3 o'clock, at noon, at Christmas |
| **on** | days, dates | on Monday, on July 4th, on my birthday |
| **in** | longer periods | in June, in 2024, in the morning |

## Other Time Prepositions

### By
**Meaning**: not later than
- *Finish **by** Friday.* (Friday or before)
- *I'll be there **by** 5 pm.*

### Until/Till
**Meaning**: up to a point in time
- *Wait **until** 6 o'clock.*
- *The shop is open **until** 9 pm.*

### For
**Meaning**: duration
- *I've lived here **for** 10 years.*
- *She waited **for** two hours.*

### Since
**Meaning**: from a point in time
- *I've known him **since** 2010.*
- *She's been here **since** Monday.*

### During
**Meaning**: within a period
- *I fell asleep **during** the movie.*

### From...to/until
- *The meeting is **from** 9 **to** 11.*
- *I work **from** Monday **to** Friday.*

## No Preposition

- *last week, next month*
- *this morning, every day*
- *yesterday, tomorrow*
