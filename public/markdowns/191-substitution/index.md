# Substitution

Substitution replaces words with **substitute forms** to avoid repetition.

## "One/Ones" for Nouns

- *I like the blue shirt. Can I try the red **one**?*
- *These shoes are nice, but those **ones** are better.*
- *Which book? The **one** on the table.*

## "Do/Does/Did" for Verb Phrases

- *She works harder than I **do**.*
- *He didn't call, but she **did**.*
- *Do you smoke? — No, I don't, but I used to [**do**].*

### "Do so" (Formal)
- *They asked me to leave, and I **did so**.*
- *If you wish to complain, please **do so** in writing.*

## "So/Not" for Clauses

### After Verbs of Thinking/Saying
- *Is it raining?* — *I think **so**.* / *I don't think **so**.*
- *Will she come?* — *I hope **so**.* / *I hope **not**.*
- *Is he guilty?* — *It seems **so**.*

### With These Verbs
- think, believe, hope, expect, suppose
- imagine, guess, be afraid
- seem, appear

## "That/This/It" for Clauses

- *She's leaving.* — *I know **that**.*
- *He lied.* — ***That** surprised me.*

## Comparison: Ellipsis vs Substitution

| Ellipsis | Substitution |
|----------|--------------|
| Omits words | Replaces with substitute |
| *She can [swim].* | *I like this one.* |
| Nothing visible | Substitute word visible |
