# Already, Still, Yet

These adverbs relate to time and completion.

## Already

**Meaning**: sooner than expected, completed
**Position**: mid-position or end
**Use**: positive sentences

- *I've **already** finished.* (sooner than expected)
- *She's **already** left.*
- *Have you eaten **already**?* (surprise)

## Yet

**Meaning**: up to now, so far
**Position**: end of sentence
**Use**: negatives and questions

- *I haven't finished **yet**.* (but I will)
- *Has she arrived **yet**?*
- *They haven't decided **yet**.*

## Still

**Meaning**: continuing, not finished
**Position**: mid-position (before main verb, after be)
**Use**: positive, negative (for surprise)

- *I'm **still** waiting.* (continuing)
- *She **still** lives there.*
- *He **still** hasn't called!* (surprise/frustration)

## Comparison

| Adverb | Position | Sentence Type |
|--------|----------|---------------|
| already | mid/end | positive |
| yet | end | negative/question |
| still | mid | positive/negative |

## Still vs Yet (Negative)

- *She hasn't arrived **yet**.* (neutral - expecting arrival)
- *She **still** hasn't arrived!* (frustrated/surprised)
