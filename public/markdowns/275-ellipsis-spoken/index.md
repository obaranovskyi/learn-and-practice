# Ellipsis in Spoken English

Leaving out words that are understood from context.

## Subject Omission

Beginning of utterance (very common):
- *(I'm) Sorry about that.*
- *(I) Can't help you.*
- *(Are you) Coming?*
- *(Do you) Want some?*
- *(I'll) See you later.*

## Auxiliary Omission

- *(Have you) Got a minute?*
- *(Have you) Seen John?*
- *(Are you) Ready?*

## Full Sentence vs Ellipsis

| Full | Ellipsis |
|------|----------|
| I don't know. | Don't know. |
| Would you like some? | Want some? |
| Are you coming? | Coming? |
| I hope so. | Hope so. |
| I have to go. | Gotta go. |

## Situational Ellipsis

When context is clear:
- "Nice day!" *(It's a nice day)*
- "Coffee?" *(Would you like some coffee?)*
- "You OK?" *(Are you OK?)*
- "Same again?" *(Would you like the same again?)*

## In Answers

- "Where's John?" - "Gone home." *(He's gone home)*
- "What's wrong?" - "Nothing." *(Nothing is wrong)*
- "Did you finish?" - "Not yet." *(I haven't finished yet)*

## Text Messages/Informal Writing

Even more ellipsis:
- "Running late" *(I'm running late)*
- "Be there soon" *(I'll be there soon)*
- "Can't make it" *(I can't make it)*

## When NOT to Use

- Formal situations
- Written English (except informal)
- When clarity is needed
