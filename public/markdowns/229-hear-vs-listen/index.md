# Hear vs Listen

## HEAR

**Passive/automatic** - perceive sound without trying

- *I can hear music.* (sound reaches my ears)
- *Did you hear that noise?*
- *I heard someone knocking.*

### Fixed expressions
- hear from (receive news): *I heard from John.*
- hear about (learn news): *I heard about the accident.*
- hear of (know of existence): *Have you heard of this band?*

### Cannot use continuous for ability
- ✅ I can hear music.
- ❌ I am hearing music. (for ability)

## LISTEN

**Active/intentional** - pay attention to sound

- listen **to**: *Listen to the teacher.*
- *I'm listening to music.* (actively choosing)
- *Are you listening to me?*

### Fixed expressions
- listen to music/radio
- listen carefully/closely
- listen up! (pay attention)

## Comparison

| Verb | Intention | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Hear | Passive | I hear a dog barking. |
| Listen | Active | I'm listening to music. |

## Examples
- *I **heard** a strange noise.* (noticed without trying)
- *I **listened** carefully to the instructions.* (paid attention)
- *Can you **hear** me?* (Is sound reaching you?)
- *Are you **listening** to me?* (Are you paying attention?)
