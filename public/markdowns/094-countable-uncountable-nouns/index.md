# Countable vs Uncountable Nouns

## Countable Nouns

Can be counted and have plural forms:
- *one book → two books*
- *a car → several cars*
- *an idea → many ideas*

**Use with**: a/an, the, numbers, many, few, a few

## Uncountable Nouns

Cannot be counted individually:
- *water, rice, information, advice*
- *music, furniture, luggage, equipment*
- *love, happiness, knowledge*

**Use with**: the (specific), some, much, little, a little

## Common Uncountable Categories

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| Liquids | water, milk, oil, coffee |
| Materials | wood, gold, plastic, paper |
| Food substances | rice, bread, sugar, flour |
| Abstract | love, information, advice, news |
| Collective | furniture, luggage, equipment |

## Nouns That Can Be Both

- *coffee* (uncountable) → *two coffees* (cups of)
- *hair* (uncountable) → *a hair* (single strand)
- *experience* (uncountable) → *an experience* (specific event)
- *time* (uncountable) → *three times* (occasions)
