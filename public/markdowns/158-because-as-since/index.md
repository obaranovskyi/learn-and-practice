# Because, As, Since (Reason)

These conjunctions express cause or reason.

## Because

**Strongest** reason word; directly answers "why?"
- *I stayed home **because** I was sick.*
- ***Because** it was raining, we stayed in.*
- *Why did you leave?* ***Because** I was tired.*

Can stand alone: *Why? **Because**!*

## As

**Softer** reason; the reason may be already known
- ***As** it was late, we decided to leave.*
- *I couldn't help **as** I was busy.*

Often at the beginning of sentences.

## Since

**Known** reason; assumes listener knows
- ***Since** you're here, can you help?*
- ***Since** we have time, let's have coffee.*
- *I'll go alone **since** you're not coming.*

## Comparison

| Conjunction | Strength | Position |
|-------------|----------|----------|
| because | strong, new info | usually after main clause |
| as | medium | often beginning |
| since | assumes known info | often beginning |

## Other Reason Words

- **due to** + noun: *Due to the rain, we stayed in.*
- **owing to** + noun: *Owing to illness, she was absent.*
- **because of** + noun: *Because of you, I'm late.*

## Note: As/Since Ambiguity

- *As* can mean "while": *As I was eating, he arrived.*
- *Since* can mean "from that time": *I've known her since 2010.*
