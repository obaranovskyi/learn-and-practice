# Quotation Marks

Quotation marks enclose direct speech and certain titles.

## Direct Speech

### American English: Double quotes
- *She said, "I'm leaving."*
- *"Hello," he said, "how are you?"*

### British English: Single quotes
- *She said, 'I'm leaving.'*
- *'Hello,' he said, 'how are you?'*

## Punctuation Placement

### American: Inside
- *"I agree," she said.*
- *He asked, "Why?"*

### British: Depends on context
- *She said, 'I agree'.*
- *Did he say 'yes'?*

## Quotes Within Quotes

Alternate between single and double:
- AmE: *She said, "He told me 'no.'"*
- BrE: *She said, 'He told me "no".'*

## Titles

Use for:
- Articles: *"The Lost City"*
- Short stories: *"The Gift of the Magi"*
- Songs: *"Imagine"*
- Poems: *"The Road Not Taken"*

Use italics for:
- Books: *The Great Gatsby*
- Movies: *Inception*
- Albums: *Abbey Road*

## Scare Quotes

Show irony or doubt:
- *The "expert" was wrong.*
- *This is their "solution."*
