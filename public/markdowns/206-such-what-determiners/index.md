# Such, What (as Determiners)

## Such

### For Emphasis
- *It was **such** a beautiful day!*
- *She's **such** a kind person.*
- *They made **such** a mess!*

### Such + a/an + adjective + singular noun
- ***Such a** nice car!*
- *I've never seen **such a** big dog.*

### Such + adjective + plural/uncountable noun
- ***Such** beautiful flowers!*
- ***Such** terrible weather!*
- *I've never heard **such** nonsense!*

### Such as (examples)
- *Fruits **such as** apples and oranges...*
- *Sports **such as** tennis and golf...*

## What (Exclamatory)

### What + a/an + noun (singular)
- ***What a** great idea!*
- ***What a** beautiful day!*
- ***What an** amazing performance!*

### What + noun (plural/uncountable)
- ***What** beautiful flowers!*
- ***What** terrible news!*
- ***What** luck!*

## Such vs What

Both express strong feeling:
- ***What a** nice day!* = ***Such a** nice day!*
- ***What** beautiful music!* = ***Such** beautiful music!*

**What** is more common in exclamations.

## Such vs So

- **such** + noun: ***Such a** tall man!*
- **so** + adjective: *He is **so** tall!*

Common mistake:
- ✗ *so a tall man*
- ✓ *such a tall man*
