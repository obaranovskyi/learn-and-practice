# Common Spelling Patterns

Rules and regularities in English spelling.

## I Before E

*"I before E, except after C"*
- bel**ie**ve, ach**ie**ve, fr**ie**nd
- rec**ei**ve, dec**ei**ve, c**ei**ling

Exceptions: weird, seize, neither, leisure, protein

## Doubling Consonants

When adding suffix (-ing, -ed, -er):
- Short vowel + single consonant → double
- run → ru**nn**ing, sit → si**tt**ing
- big → bi**gg**er, stop → sto**pp**ed

Don't double:
- Long vowel: read → reading
- Two consonants: jump → jumping
- Unstressed final syllable: open → opening

## Drop the E

Before vowel suffixes:
- make → making (not makeing)
- hope → hoping, love → loving

Keep E before consonant suffixes:
- hope → hopeful, care → careful

## Y to I

Before suffixes (except -ing):
- happy → happ**i**ness, happ**i**er
- carry → carr**i**ed (but: carrying)
- beauty → beaut**i**ful

Keep Y after vowel:
- play → played, enjoy → enjoyed

## -FUL (One L)

- beautiful, careful, wonderful, helpful
- Not: beautifull ❌

## -TION / -SION

- action, nation, education (-tion)
- division, television (-sion after consonant)
- confusion, explosion (-sion after vowel)
