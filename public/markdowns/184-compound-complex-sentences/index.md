# Compound-Complex Sentences

A compound-complex sentence has **at least two independent clauses** and **at least one dependent clause**.

## Structure

**Independent + Independent + Dependent**
(in any arrangement)

## Examples

- ***When the rain started** [D], **I ran inside** [I], **but my friend stayed outside** [I].*
- ***She studied hard** [I], **so she passed** [I], **although it was difficult** [D].*
- ***If you're ready** [D], **we can leave** [I], **or we can wait** [I].*

## Breaking It Down

Example: *When he called, I answered, but she didn't.*

| Clause | Type |
|--------|------|
| When he called | Dependent (adverb) |
| I answered | Independent |
| but she didn't | Independent |

## Requirements

Must have:
- ✓ At least 2 independent clauses
- ✓ At least 1 dependent clause

## Comparison

| Type | Structure |
|------|-----------|
| Simple | 1 independent |
| Compound | 2+ independent |
| Complex | 1 independent + 1+ dependent |
| Compound-Complex | 2+ independent + 1+ dependent |

## Tips for Writing

1. Don't make sentences too long
2. Use proper punctuation
3. Make sure meaning is clear
