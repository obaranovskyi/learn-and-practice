# Vague Language

Deliberately imprecise language common in spoken English.

## Softening/Hedging

- *kind of* / *kinda* - "I'm kind of tired."
- *sort of* / *sorta* - "It's sort of blue."
- *a bit* - "I'm a bit worried."
- *fairly* / *pretty* - "It's fairly expensive."
- *more or less* - "It's more or less finished."

## Vague Quantities

- *about* / *around* - "about 20 people"
- *roughly* - "roughly $100"
- *or so* - "ten minutes or so"
- *some* / *a few* - "some problems"
- *loads of* / *tons of* - "tons of work"

## Vague Categories

- *and stuff (like that)* - "books and stuff"
- *and things (like that)* - "food and things"
- *or something (like that)* - "a doctor or something"
- *whatever* - "fruit, vegetables, whatever"
- *and so on* - "pens, paper, and so on"

## Vague Nouns

- *thing(s)* - "Where's the thing?"
- *stuff* - "What's that stuff?"
- *thingy* / *whatchamacallit* - forgotten names

## Functions

### Politeness
- Direct: "You're wrong."
- Vague: "You're sort of wrong."

### Approximation
- "It cost around $50."

### Unknown information
- "He's a lawyer or something."

### Avoiding overgeneralization
- "I kind of like it." (= not completely)

## When to Avoid

- Formal writing
- Academic papers
- Official documents
- When precision matters
