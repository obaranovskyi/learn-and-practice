# Compound Adjectives

Compound adjectives combine two or more words, usually with hyphens.

## Common Patterns

### Number + Noun
- *a **five-star** hotel*
- *a **three-hour** flight*
- *a **ten-year-old** boy*
- *a **two-week** vacation*

Note: The noun stays singular!
- ✓ *a five-year plan*
- ✗ *a five-years plan*

### Adjective + Noun
- *a **full-time** job*
- *a **last-minute** decision*
- *a **high-quality** product*
- ***long-term** effects*

### Adjective + Past Participle
- *a **well-known** author*
- *a **good-looking** man*
- *a **narrow-minded** person*
- *an **open-minded** approach*

### Noun + Past Participle
- *a **hand-made** gift*
- *a **sun-dried** tomato*
- *a **home-cooked** meal*
- ***factory-produced** goods*

### Adjective + Present Participle
- *a **good-looking** woman*
- *a **hard-working** student*
- *an **easy-going** person*
- *a **long-lasting** effect*

## When to Hyphenate

- **Before the noun**: *a well-known actor*
- **After the noun** (no hyphen): *The actor is well known.*
