# Too vs Very vs Enough

These words all express degree but with different implications.

## Very

**Meaning**: high degree (neutral, no problem)
**Position**: before adjective/adverb

- *It's **very** cold.* (just describing)
- *She's **very** tall.*
- *He runs **very** fast.*

## Too

**Meaning**: excessive, more than needed (negative - problem exists)
**Position**: before adjective/adverb

- *It's **too** cold to swim.* (problem!)
- *She's **too** young to drive.*
- *He talks **too** fast.*

### Too + Adjective + To + Infinitive
- *I'm **too tired to** work.*
- *It's **too heavy to** lift.*

### Too + Adjective + For + Someone
- *It's **too difficult for** me.*
- *The music is **too loud for** the baby.*

## Enough

**Meaning**: sufficient, as much as needed
**Position**: AFTER adjective/adverb, BEFORE noun

- *She's old **enough** to vote.* (after adjective)
- *He doesn't run fast **enough**.* (after adverb)
- *I have **enough** money.* (before noun)

### Enough + To + Infinitive
- *She's smart **enough to** understand.*
- *He's not tall **enough to** reach.*

## Comparison

| Word | Position | Meaning |
|------|----------|---------|
| very | before adj | high degree |
| too | before adj | excessive (problem) |
| enough | after adj | sufficient |
