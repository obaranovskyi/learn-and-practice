# Either...or

"Either...or" presents two alternatives or choices.

## Basic Usage

- *You can have **either** coffee **or** tea.*
- ***Either** you apologize **or** I leave.*
- *She's **either** in her office **or** in the meeting.*

## Meaning

One or the other (not both):
- ***Either** John **or** Mary will come.* (one of them)
- *Take **either** the bus **or** the train.*

## With Different Elements

### Nouns
*Choose **either** the red one **or** the blue one.*

### Verbs
*We can **either** go out **or** stay home.*

### Clauses
***Either** you're with us, **or** you're against us.*

## Subject-Verb Agreement

Verb agrees with the **nearer** subject:
- ***Either** you **or** he **is** wrong.* (he = singular)
- ***Either** he **or** you **are** wrong.* (you = plural)
- ***Either** the cat **or** the dogs **are** outside.* (dogs = plural)

## "Either" Alone

As determiner/pronoun:
- ***Either** option is fine.* (one or the other)
- *I don't like **either** of them.*
