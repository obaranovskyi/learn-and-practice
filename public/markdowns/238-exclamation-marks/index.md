# Exclamation Marks

Exclamation marks (!) express strong emotion or emphasis.

## Exclamations
- *What a beautiful day!*
- *How wonderful!*
- *What an amazing view!*

## Strong Emotion
- *I can't believe it!*
- *That's incredible!*
- *I love it!*

## Commands (urgent)
- *Stop!*
- *Watch out!*
- *Help!*

## Interjections
- *Wow!*
- *Oh no!*
- *Congratulations!*

## With Quotation Marks
- *She shouted, "Run!"*
- *"Fantastic!" he exclaimed.*

## Overuse Warning

Don't overuse - they lose impact:
- ❌ *I went to the store! I bought milk! It was great!*
- ✅ *I went to the store and bought milk. It was a nice day.*

## In Formal Writing

Avoid in:
- Academic papers
- Business letters
- Professional reports

## Multiple Exclamation Marks

Generally avoid in formal writing:
- ❌ *That's amazing!!!*
- ✅ *That's amazing!*

## Interrobang (!?)

For shocked questions (informal):
- *You did what?!*
- *She said that!?*
