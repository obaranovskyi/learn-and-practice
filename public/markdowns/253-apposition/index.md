# Apposition

Placing two noun phrases side by side, where one explains the other.

## Basic Structure

- *My friend **John** is coming.* (restrictive)
- *John, **my best friend**, is coming.* (non-restrictive)

## Restrictive Apposition (No Commas)

Identifies which one:
- *The poet **Burns** wrote beautiful poems.*
- *My brother **Tom** lives in London.* (implies other brothers)
- *The year **2020** was difficult.*

## Non-Restrictive Apposition (With Commas)

Adds extra information:
- *Paris, **the capital of France**, is beautiful.*
- *My mother, **a retired teacher**, loves gardening.*
- *Einstein, **a famous physicist**, changed science.*

## Types of Apposition

### Equivalent
- *The CEO, **Mr. Smith**, resigned.*

### Specifying
- *The city **of London** is huge.*
- *The concept **of freedom** is important.*

### Attributive
- *King **Henry VIII** had six wives.*
- *President **Biden** spoke today.*

## Sentence Appositives

Explains whole idea:
- *She passed all her exams, **a remarkable achievement**.*
- *He quit his job, **a decision he later regretted**.*

## Punctuation

- Restrictive: no commas
- Non-restrictive: commas (or dashes/parentheses)
