# Fronting

Fronting moves an element to the **beginning** of a sentence for emphasis.

## Fronting Objects

Normal: *I like **this book**.*
Fronted: ***This book** I like.* (emphatic)

Normal: *I can't stand **that noise**.*
Fronted: ***That noise** I can't stand.*

## Fronting Complements

Normal: *The view was **beautiful**.*
Fronted: ***Beautiful** was the view.*

Normal: *He is **honest**.*
Fronted: ***Honest** he is.* (emphatic)

## Fronting Adverbials

Normal: *A strange figure stood **in the doorway**.*
Fronted: ***In the doorway** stood a strange figure.*

Normal: *She went **away**.*
Fronted: ***Away** she went.*

## Fronting for Contrast

- ***This one** I'll keep, **that one** I'll throw away.*
- ***Some people** I like, **others** I don't.*

## Fronting Verb Phrases (Rare)

- *She said she'd help, and **help she did**.*
- *They wanted to win, and **win they did**.*

## Common Patterns

| Normal | Fronted |
|--------|---------|
| I like this. | This I like. |
| She is smart. | Smart she is. |
| He stood there. | There he stood. |

## Notes

- Used for emphasis or literary effect
- More common in writing than speech
- Creates dramatic or formal tone
