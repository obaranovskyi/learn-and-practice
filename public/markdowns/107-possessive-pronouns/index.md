# Possessive Pronouns

Possessive pronouns replace a possessive adjective + noun.

## Possessive Pronouns

| Person | Possessive Adjective | Possessive Pronoun |
|--------|---------------------|-------------------|
| I | my | mine |
| you | your | yours |
| he | his | his |
| she | her | hers |
| it | its | (its - rare) |
| we | our | ours |
| they | their | theirs |

## Usage

**Stand alone** (no noun after):
- *This book is **mine**.* (not: my book)
- *Is this pen **yours**?*
- *That car is **hers**.*
- ***Theirs** is bigger than **ours**.*

## Key Difference

| Possessive Adjective | Possessive Pronoun |
|---------------------|-------------------|
| This is **my** book. | This book is **mine**. |
| That's **her** car. | That car is **hers**. |
| **Our** house is big. | **Ours** is big. |

## No Apostrophe!

- ✗ *your's, her's, their's, our's*
- ✓ *yours, hers, theirs, ours*

## Common Patterns

- *A friend of mine* (not: a friend of me)
- *That idea of yours*
- *Some colleagues of hers*
