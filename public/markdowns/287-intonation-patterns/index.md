# Intonation Patterns

The rise and fall of voice pitch.

## Falling Intonation ↘

### Statements
- "I live in London." ↘
- "She's a teacher." ↘

### Wh- Questions
- "Where do you live?" ↘
- "What's your name?" ↘

### Commands
- "Close the door." ↘
- "Sit down." ↘

### Exclamations
- "What a beautiful day!" ↘
- "That's amazing!" ↘

## Rising Intonation ↗

### Yes/No Questions
- "Are you coming?" ↗
- "Do you like coffee?" ↗

### Checking/Confirming
- "You're from Japan?" ↗
- "Tomorrow at 3?" ↗

### Lists (not final item)
- "I bought apples↗, oranges↗, and bananas↘."

### Politeness/Uncertainty
- "Could you help me?" ↗
- "Maybe..." ↗

## Fall-Rise ↘↗

### Contrast/Correction
- "I didn't say that..." ↘↗ (implying "but...")

### Politeness/Hesitation
- "Well..." ↘↗
- "Actually..." ↘↗

## Question Tags

- Seeking information: ↗ "You're coming, aren't you?" ↗
- Expecting agreement: ↘ "Nice day, isn't it?" ↘

## Attitude and Meaning

Intonation conveys:
- Certainty vs uncertainty
- Interest vs boredom
- Politeness vs rudeness
- Completion vs continuation

## Example Differences

"Really" with different intonations:
- Really? ↗ (surprise, interest)
- Really. ↘ (I know, obvious)
- Really... ↘↗ (doubt)
