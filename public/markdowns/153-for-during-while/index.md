# For vs During vs While

## For

**Meaning**: how long (duration)
**Followed by**: period of time

- *I lived there **for** five years.*
- *She waited **for** two hours.*
- ***For** a long time, I didn't know.*

**Used with**: numbers + time words
- *for 10 minutes, for 3 days, for a week*

## During

**Meaning**: when (within a period)
**Followed by**: noun/event

- *I fell asleep **during** the movie.*
- *It happened **during** the war.*
- ***During** my vacation, I relaxed.*

**Used with**: named periods/events
- *during the meeting, during summer, during class*

## While

**Meaning**: when (at the same time as)
**Followed by**: subject + verb (clause)

- *I fell asleep **while** I was watching TV.*
- ***While** we were eating, she called.*
- *Don't talk **while** you're eating.*

## Comparison

| For | During | While |
|-----|--------|-------|
| **for** 3 hours | **during** the meeting | **while** we waited |
| how long | which event | same time as |
| + time period | + noun | + clause |

## Common Mistakes

- ✗ *during 3 hours*
- ✓ *for 3 hours*

- ✗ *during I was sleeping*
- ✓ *while I was sleeping*
