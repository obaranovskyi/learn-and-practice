# Possessive Adjectives

Possessive adjectives show ownership and come before nouns.

## Possessive Adjectives

| Subject | Possessive Adjective |
|---------|---------------------|
| I | my |
| you | your |
| he | his |
| she | her |
| it | its |
| we | our |
| they | their |

## Usage

Always followed by a noun:
- ***My** car is red.*
- *This is **her** book.*
- *The dog wagged **its** tail.*
- ***Their** house is beautiful.*

## Its vs It's

- **its** = possessive: *The cat licked its paw.*
- **it's** = it is/it has: *It's raining.*

## Their vs There vs They're

- **their** = possessive: *their car*
- **there** = place: *over there*
- **they're** = they are: *They're coming.*

## Your vs You're

- **your** = possessive: *your book*
- **you're** = you are: *You're right.*

## No Articles With

- ✗ *the my book*
- ✓ *my book*
