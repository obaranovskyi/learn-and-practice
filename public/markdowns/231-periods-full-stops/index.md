# Periods / Full Stops

The period (AmE) or full stop (BrE) ends declarative sentences.

## Main Uses

### End statements
- *I like coffee.*
- *She went to the store.*
- *The meeting is at 3 PM.*

### Abbreviations
- Mr. / Mrs. / Ms. / Dr.
- etc. / e.g. / i.e.
- a.m. / p.m.
- U.S.A. / U.K.

**Note**: British English often omits periods in abbreviations:
- Mr Smith, Dr Jones

## When NOT to Use

### After titles
- ❌ The Great Gatsby.
- ✅ The Great Gatsby

### After questions
- ❌ What time is it.
- ✅ What time is it?

### After exclamations
- ❌ What a beautiful day.
- ✅ What a beautiful day!

### In bulleted lists (optional)
- Item one
- Item two
- Item three

## With Quotation Marks

**American**: Period inside quotes
- She said, "I'm leaving."

**British**: Period outside (unless part of quote)
- She said, "I'm leaving".
