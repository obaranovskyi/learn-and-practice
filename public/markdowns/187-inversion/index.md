# Inversion

Inversion means putting the **verb before the subject**.

## Question Inversion

### Yes/No Questions
- *She **is** happy.* → ***Is** she happy?*
- *You **can** swim.* → ***Can** you swim?*
- *They **have** left.* → ***Have** they left?*

### Wh-Questions
- ***Where is** she?*
- ***What did** you say?*

## After Negative Adverbs

When negative/restrictive adverbs start a sentence:

- ***Never have** I seen such beauty.*
- ***Rarely does** he complain.*
- ***Seldom do** we eat out.*
- ***Not only did** he win, but he broke the record.*
- ***Hardly had** I arrived when it started raining.*
- ***Little did** I know what would happen.*

## After "So/Such/Neither/Nor"

- *She's tired. **So am** I.* (= I am too)
- *I can't swim. **Neither can** he.* (= he can't either)
- ***Such was** his anger that he left.*

## Conditional Without "If"

- ***Had** I known, I would have helped.* (= If I had known)
- ***Were** she here, she'd agree.* (= If she were here)
- ***Should** you need help, call me.* (= If you should need)

## After "Here/There"

- ***Here comes** the bus!*
- ***There goes** my chance!*

(But not with pronouns: *Here **it** comes!* ✓)
