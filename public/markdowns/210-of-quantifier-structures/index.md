# Of + Quantifier Structures

Using "of" with quantifiers to refer to specific groups.

## When to Use "Of"

Before **determiners** (the, my, these, those, etc.):
- ***Most of the** students passed.*
- ***Some of my** friends came.*
- ***All of these** books are mine.*
- ***None of those** answers is correct.*

## Without "Of"

Before **bare nouns**:
- ***Most** students passed.* (general)
- ***Some** friends came.* (general)
- ***All** books have pages.* (general)

## Comparison

| With "of" (specific) | Without "of" (general) |
|---------------------|------------------------|
| Most of the students | Most students |
| Some of my friends | Some friends |
| All of these books | All books |

## Verb Agreement

Verb agrees with the noun **after "of"**:
- *Most of the water **is** clean.* (uncountable → singular)
- *Most of the students **are** here.* (plural → plural)
- *Some of the cake **is** left.*
- *Some of the cookies **are** gone.*

## Common Patterns

- *all of, most of, some of, none of*
- *both of, each of, either of, neither of*
- *many of, much of, few of, little of*
- *half of, a third of, two-thirds of*

## "Of" Optional with All/Both/Half

- ***All (of)** the students...*
- ***Both (of)** my parents...*
- ***Half (of)** the cake...*

## Must Use "Of"

Before pronouns:
- ***All of** us / **some of** them / **none of** it*
- ✗ *All us, some them*
