# Possessive Nouns ('s vs s')

Possessive forms show ownership or relationship.

## Rules

### Singular Nouns: Add 's
- *the dog's tail*
- *John's car*
- *the boss's office*
- *James's book* (or James')

### Plural Nouns ending in -s: Add '
- *the dogs' tails* (multiple dogs)
- *the students' books*
- *my parents' house*

### Irregular Plurals: Add 's
- *the children's toys*
- *the men's room*
- *the women's team*

## 's vs of

**Use 's for**:
- People: *Mary's job*
- Animals: *the cat's food*
- Time: *today's news, a week's holiday*
- Places: *London's traffic*

**Use "of" for**:
- Things: *the door of the house*
- Abstract: *the end of the story*

## Joint vs Separate Possession

- *Jack and Jill's house* (shared house)
- *Jack's and Jill's cars* (separate cars)

## Common Expressions

- *at the doctor's* (doctor's office)
- *at my grandmother's* (grandmother's house)
- *a stone's throw away*
