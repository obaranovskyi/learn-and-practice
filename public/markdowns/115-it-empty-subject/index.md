# It as Empty Subject

English requires a subject, so **"it"** fills this role when there's no real subject.

## Weather

- ***It's** raining.*
- ***It's** sunny/cloudy/windy.*
- ***It** snowed yesterday.*
- ***It's** going to be cold.*

## Time

- *What time is **it**? **It's** 3 o'clock.*
- ***It's** Monday.*
- ***It's** January.*
- ***It's** early/late.*

## Distance

- *How far is **it**? **It's** 5 kilometers.*
- ***It's** a long way to Tokyo.*

## General Situations

- ***It's** difficult to learn Chinese.*
- ***It** seems that he's right.*
- ***It's** important to exercise.*
- ***It** takes two hours to get there.*

## It vs There

| It (identity/weather/time) | There (existence) |
|---------------------------|-------------------|
| *It's cold.* | *There's a cat outside.* |
| *It's 5 km to town.* | *There are 5 km to go.* |
| *It's difficult.* | *There's a problem.* |

## Cleft Sentences (Emphasis)

- ***It** was John who broke the window.*
- ***It's** the price that I don't like.*
