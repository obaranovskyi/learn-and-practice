# Quantifiers for Both Countable & Uncountable

These quantifiers work with **both** noun types.

## Some / Any

- *I have **some** friends.* (countable)
- *I have **some** money.* (uncountable)
- *Do you have **any** questions?* (countable)
- *Is there **any** water?* (uncountable)

## A lot of / Lots of

- ***A lot of** people came.* (countable)
- ***A lot of** time was wasted.* (uncountable)
- *There are **lots of** reasons.* (countable)
- *There's **lots of** space.* (uncountable)

## Plenty of

- ***Plenty of** chairs are available.* (countable)
- ***Plenty of** room is left.* (uncountable)

## Enough

- *We have **enough** chairs.* (countable)
- *There's **enough** time.* (uncountable)

## No / None

- *There are **no** cars.* (countable)
- *There is **no** water.* (uncountable)
- ***None** of the students came.* (countable)
- ***None** of the information is useful.* (uncountable)

## All / Most

- ***All/Most** students passed.* (countable)
- ***All/Most** water is clean.* (uncountable)

## Summary Table

| Works with Both | Examples |
|-----------------|----------|
| some, any | some books, some water |

| plenty of | plenty of chairs, plenty of room |
