# All, Both, Whole, Every, Each

## All

For **three or more** items:
- ***All** students passed.* (= every one)
- ***All** the water is gone.*
- ***All of** them came.*

Position: before articles/possessives
- ***All** my friends*
- ***All** the books*

## Both

For **exactly two**:
- ***Both** sisters are here.*
- ***Both of** them came.*
- *They **both** agreed.*

## Whole

Emphasizes **completeness** (singular nouns):
- *I ate the **whole** cake.*
- *She read the **whole** book.*
- *The **whole** class passed.*

**All** vs **Whole**: 
- *all the cake* = *the whole cake*
- *all my friends* (✓) but *my whole friends* (✗)

## Every

For **all members** of group (singular):
- ***Every** student has a book.*
- ***Every** day is a new chance.*
- Not: *every of them* (✗)

## Each

Emphasizes **individuals** (singular):
- ***Each** student has a book.*
- ***Each of** them has one.*
- *They have two **each**.*

### Each vs Every

| Each | Every |
|------|-------|
| Individual | All members |
| 2+ items | 3+ items |
| Can use "of" | Cannot use "of" |
