# Ellipsis

Ellipsis is **omitting words** that can be understood from context.

## In Compound Structures

### Omitting Repeated Verbs
- *She can sing and [she can] dance.*
- *I will call you or [I will] text you.*

### Omitting Repeated Subjects
- *He came in and [he] sat down.*
- *She smiled but [she] didn't speak.*

## In Comparisons

- *She's taller than I [am tall].*
- *He works harder than she [works].*
- *I have more books than you [have].*

## In Short Answers

- *Are you coming?* — *Yes, I am [coming].*
- *Can she swim?* — *Yes, she can [swim].*
- *Did they leave?* — *No, they didn't [leave].*

## After "to" (Infinitive)

- *Are you coming?* — *I'd like to [come].*
- *Can you help?* — *I'll try to [help].*
- *You can leave if you want to [leave].*

## In "So" and "Not" Structures

- *I think so.* (= I think [that's true])
- *I hope not.* (= I hope [that's not true])
- *I'm afraid so/not.*

## In Relative Clauses

- *The book [that/which] I read was good.*
- *The man [who/that] I met was kind.*

## When to Use Ellipsis

- To avoid repetition
- To make speech/writing more natural
- When meaning is clear
