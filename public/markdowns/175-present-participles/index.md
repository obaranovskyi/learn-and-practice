# Present Participles (V-ing)

The present participle is formed by adding **-ing** to the base verb.

## Formation

- *walk → walking*
- *run → running* (double consonant)
- *write → writing* (drop -e)
- *lie → lying* (ie → y)

## Uses

### 1. Continuous Tenses
- *She **is working**.*
- *They **were playing**.*
- *I **have been waiting**.*

### 2. As Adjectives
- *an **interesting** book*
- *a **boring** movie*
- *the **running** water*

### 3. After Verbs of Perception
- *I saw him **leaving**.*
- *She heard someone **singing**.*
- *We watched them **playing**.*

### 4. After "go" (activities)
- *go **swimming**, go **shopping***
- *go **running**, go **dancing***

### 5. In Participle Clauses
- ***Walking** home, I saw an accident.*
- ***Being** tired, she went to bed early.*

### 6. After "spend/waste time"
- *He **spent** hours **working**.*
- *Don't **waste** time **worrying**.*

## Present Participle vs Gerund

Both are -ing forms, but:
- **Gerund** = noun function: *Swimming is fun.*
- **Participle** = adjective/verb function: *The swimming pool*
