# Few, A few, Little, A little

## With "A" = Positive (some)

### A few (countable)
- *I have **a few** friends.* (some, enough)
- *She made **a few** mistakes.* (not many, but OK)

### A little (uncountable)
- *I have **a little** money.* (some, enough)
- *Would you like **a little** more?*

## Without "A" = Negative (not enough)

### Few (countable)
- *I have **few** friends.* (not many, not enough)
- ***Few** people understand this.* (hardly any)

### Little (uncountable)
- *I have **little** money.* (not much, not enough)
- *There's **little** hope.* (almost none)

## Comparison

| Word | Type | Meaning |
|------|------|---------|
| **a few** | countable | some (positive) |
| **few** | countable | not many (negative) |
| **a little** | uncountable | some (positive) |
| **little** | uncountable | not much (negative) |

## Examples

- *I have **a few** minutes.* (= I can help)
- *I have **few** minutes.* (= I'm busy)
- *There's **a little** cake left.* (= you can have some)
- *There's **little** cake left.* (= almost none)

## "Quite a few" / "Quite a little"

**Quite a few** = a good number (more than expected)
- *Quite a few people came.*

## Very Few / Very Little

Emphasizes the negative:
- ***Very few** people know this.*
- *There's **very little** time.*
