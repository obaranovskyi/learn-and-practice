# See vs Look vs Watch

## SEE

**Passive/automatic** - notice with your eyes (not intentional)

- *I can see the mountains from here.*
- *Did you see what happened?*
- *I saw a bird in the garden.*

### Fixed expressions
- see a doctor/lawyer
- see a film/movie (at cinema)
- see the sights
- see someone home
- I see (understand)

## LOOK

**Active/intentional** - direct your eyes toward something (brief)

- look **at**: *Look at this photo!*
- look **for**: *I'm looking for my keys.*
- look **like**: *You look like your mother.*

### Fixed expressions
- look after (care for)
- look forward to
- look up (in dictionary)
- look out! (be careful)

## WATCH

**Active/intentional** - observe something **moving/changing** over time

- *Watch TV/a game/a movie (at home)*
- *Watch out! (be careful)*
- *I watched the children playing.*

### Fixed expressions
- watch out (be careful)
- watch your step
- bird watching

## Summary

| Verb | Intention | Duration |
|------|-----------|----------|
| See | Passive | Instant |
| Look | Active | Brief |
| Watch | Active | Extended |

## Examples
- *I **saw** a car accident.* (noticed)
- *I **looked** at the car.* (directed eyes briefly)
- *I **watched** the cars racing.* (observed over time)
