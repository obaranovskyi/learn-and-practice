# Get-passive vs Be-passive

## Get + past participle (informal)
Often implies:
- Something negative or unexpected
- Subject's involvement
- Change of state

Examples:
- He **got fired**. (negative event)
- She **got married**. (change of state)
- I **got hit** by a car.

## Be + past participle (neutral/formal)
Standard passive, no extra implications.
- He **was fired**.
- She **was married** in June.

## Common Get-passives
get married, get divorced, get hurt, get killed, get lost, get stuck, get dressed
