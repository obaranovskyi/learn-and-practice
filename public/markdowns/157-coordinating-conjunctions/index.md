# Coordinating Conjunctions (FANBOYS)

Coordinating conjunctions join equal elements: words, phrases, or independent clauses.

## FANBOYS

| Conjunction | Function | Example |
|-------------|----------|---------|
| **F**or | reason | *I stayed home, **for** it was raining.* |
| **A**nd | addition | *She sings **and** dances.* |
| **N**or | negative addition | *I don't eat meat, **nor** do I eat fish.* |
| **B**ut | contrast | *It's small **but** comfortable.* |
| **O**r | alternative | *Coffee **or** tea?* |
| **Y**et | contrast (surprising) | *He's rich, **yet** he's unhappy.* |
| **S**o | result | *It rained, **so** we stayed in.* |

## Usage

### AND (addition)
*She's smart **and** hardworking.*

### BUT (contrast)
*I tried, **but** I failed.*

### OR (alternative/choice)
*Do you want this **or** that?*

### SO (result/consequence)
*I was tired, **so** I went to bed.*

### YET (contrast - surprising)
*It's cheap, **yet** high quality.*

### FOR (reason - formal)
*He must be home, **for** his car is here.*

### NOR (negative alternative)
*She doesn't smoke, **nor** does she drink.*

## Punctuation

- **Comma before** conjunction when joining independent clauses
- *I like coffee, **but** she prefers tea.*
- No comma for short phrases: *I like coffee but not tea.*
