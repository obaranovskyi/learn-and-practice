# Much, Many, A lot of, Lots of, Plenty of

## Much (uncountable)

Mainly in **negatives** and **questions**:
- *I don't have **much** time.*
- *Is there **much** traffic?*
- *How **much** money do you need?*

In positives (formal): *There is **much** to discuss.*

## Many (countable)

Negatives, questions, and positives:
- *There aren't **many** people.*
- *Do you have **many** friends?*
- ***Many** students attended.*

## A lot of / Lots of

Both countable and uncountable, mainly **positive**:
- *She has **a lot of** money.* (uncountable)
- *She has **a lot of** friends.* (countable)
- *There are **lots of** reasons.*

More informal than "much/many" in positives.

## Plenty of

**More than enough**:
- *We have **plenty of** time.*
- *There are **plenty of** options.*

## Summary

| Word | Countable | Uncountable | Sentence Type |
|------|-----------|-------------|---------------|
| many | ✓ | ✗ | all |
| much | ✗ | ✓ | mainly neg/quest |
| a lot of | ✓ | ✓ | mainly positive |
| lots of | ✓ | ✓ | positive, informal |
| plenty of | ✓ | ✓ | positive |

## "A lot" Without Noun

- *I like it **a lot**.* (adverb)
- *Thanks **a lot**.*
