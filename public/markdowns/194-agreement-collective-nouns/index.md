# Agreement with Collective Nouns

Collective nouns can be singular or plural depending on meaning.

## Common Collective Nouns

*team, family, committee, government, staff, audience, class, group, jury, public, company, crowd*

## As a Unit (Singular)

When acting as **one entity**:
- *The team **is** winning.*
- *My family **is** large.*
- *The committee **has** decided.*
- *The audience **was** quiet.*

## As Individuals (Plural)

When members act **separately** (more common in BrE):
- *The team **are** arguing among themselves.*
- *The family **have** different opinions.*
- *The committee **are** divided.*

## American vs British English

| Context | AmE | BrE |
|---------|-----|-----|
| Unit | singular | singular |
| Individuals | usually singular | often plural |

AmE: *The team **is** arguing.*
BrE: *The team **are** arguing.*

## Consistency

Be consistent within a sentence:
- ✓ *The team **is** playing **its** best.*
- ✓ *The team **are** playing **their** best.*
- ✗ *The team **is** playing **their** best.*

## Special Cases

Always plural:
- *The police **are** investigating.*
- *People **are** waiting.*

Always singular:
- *The news **is** good.*
- *The United States **is** large.*
