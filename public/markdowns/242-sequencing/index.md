# Sequencing

Sequencing words order ideas and events.

## Beginning
- **First/Firstly**: *First, preheat the oven.*
- **To begin with**: *To begin with, let me explain the rules.*
- **Initially/At first**: *At first, it seemed easy.*
- **First of all**: *First of all, we need a plan.*

## Middle
- **Second/Secondly/Thirdly**: *Secondly, add the flour.*
- **Then/Next/After that**: *Then, mix the ingredients.*
- **Subsequently**: *Subsequently, the project was approved.*
- **Meanwhile/In the meantime**: *Meanwhile, prepare the sauce.*

## End
- **Finally/Lastly**: *Finally, serve hot.*
- **In the end/Eventually**: *In the end, we succeeded.*
- **Last but not least**: *Last but not least, thank the team.*
- **To conclude**: *To conclude, this was a success.*

## Simultaneous Actions
- **At the same time**: *At the same time, stir the sauce.*
- **Meanwhile**: *Meanwhile, in another room...*
- **While**: *While waiting, I read a book.*

## Formal vs Informal
- Formal: *Firstly... Secondly... Thirdly...*
- Informal: *First... Then... After that...*
