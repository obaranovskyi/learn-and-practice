# Both...and

"Both...and" emphasizes that two things are equally true or included.

## Basic Usage

- ***Both** coffee **and** tea are served.*
- *She is **both** smart **and** hardworking.*
- *He speaks **both** English **and** French.*
- *I like **both** cats **and** dogs.*

## With Different Elements

### Nouns
*I enjoy **both** music **and** art.*

### Adjectives
*The house is **both** large **and** comfortable.*

### Verbs
*She **both** sings **and** dances.*

### Phrases
*He's successful **both** at work **and** at home.*

## Parallel Structure

Keep elements grammatically parallel:
- ✓ *She **both** cooks **and** cleans.* (verb + verb)
- ✓ *She is **both** a teacher **and** a writer.* (noun + noun)
- ✗ *She **both** cooks **and** is cleaning.* (not parallel)

## Subject-Verb Agreement

With "both...and", use **plural** verb:
- ***Both** the teacher **and** the student **are** here.*
- ***Both** he **and** I **were** invited.*

## Position

"Both" goes before the first element:
- ✓ *I like **both** coffee **and** tea.*
- ✗ *I **both** like coffee **and** tea.* (awkward)
