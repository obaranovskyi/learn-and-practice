# Indefinite Articles "a/an"

**"A"** and **"an"** are used for non-specific singular countable nouns.

## A vs An

- **"a"** before consonant sounds: *a book, a university, a European*
- **"an"** before vowel sounds: *an apple, an hour, an honest person*

## When to Use

1. **First mention**: *I saw a dog in the park.*
2. **One of many**: *She's a doctor.* (one of many doctors)
3. **Job/profession**: *He's an engineer.*
4. **With expressions of quantity**: *a few, a little, a lot of*
5. **Exclamations with what**: *What a beautiful day!*
6. **Per/each meaning**: *twice a week, $50 an hour*

## Examples

- *I need a pen.* (any pen)
- *She bought an umbrella.*
- *He's an honest man.*
- *A dog is a loyal animal.* (general statement)
