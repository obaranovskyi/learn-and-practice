# Adverbs of Time

Adverbs of time tell us **when** something happens.

## Categories

### Present
- *now, today, right now*
- *at the moment, currently*

### Past
- *yesterday, last week/month/year*
- *ago, before, earlier*
- *recently, lately, just*

### Future
- *tomorrow, soon, later*
- *next week/month/year*
- *eventually, shortly*

### Duration
- *always, forever, permanently*
- *temporarily, briefly*

### Frequency-related
- *once, twice, again*
- *first, then, finally*

## Position

**Usually at the end** of a sentence:
- *I saw him **yesterday**.*
- *She'll arrive **soon**.*

**At the beginning** for emphasis:
- ***Tomorrow**, we're leaving early.*
- ***Yesterday**, I met an old friend.*

## Order of Time Adverbs

When using multiple: **duration → frequency → time**
- *She worked hard **all day long** **twice** **last week**.*
